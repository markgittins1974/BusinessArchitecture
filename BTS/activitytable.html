Activity Reference

Activity # Activity Name Description Pain Point User Role
VALUE STAGE 1: INTAKE & VALIDATION
1.1 Receive PIC / Trigger Receive a PIC, referral, anomaly, or system-generated trigger from internal or external sources PICs come from everywhere — no consistency. Multiple intake channels create inconsistent PIC quality. Investigator, Team Leader, Third-Party Data Provider
1.1-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception Council valuation anomalies, land use mismatches, vacant residential land indicators, trust ownership irregularities Council data is always late or inconsistent. Investigator, Team Leader
1.1-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception ATO STP discrepancies, VWA wage declaration mismatch, grouping anomalies, large payroll growth, contractor flags STP data is messy — it never lines up with what employers report. Investigator, Team Leader
1.1-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception Suspicious grant applications, builder anomalies, high-risk postcodes, identity mismatch alerts, occupancy anomalies FHOG fraud is creative — people try everything. Investigator, Team Leader
1.1-Duties ◇ Duties Exception Conveyancer referral anomalies, property undervaluation, foreign purchaser flags, related-party transfers, trust structures Conveyancers don't always give us the full story. Investigator, Team Leader
1.2 Validate Trigger Determine whether the trigger meets the threshold for an investigation or should be redirected Half the PICs shouldn't even be investigations. Lack of clear intake criteria leads to misrouted work. Investigator, Team Leader, Legal / Policy Advisor
1.3 Create/Update Case Record Open or update the case record, attach documents, and ensure metadata is correct We enter the same info in three places. Duplicate data entry across ESYS, CM9, and email increases administrative load. Investigator, Team Leader, Processor
VALUE STAGE 2: TRIAGE & PRIORITISATION
2.1 Initial Checks & Background Searches Perform quick checks to understand the customer, entity structure, history, and potential risk indicators We spend half our time Googling. Lack of integrated data sources forces manual searching across multiple systems. Investigator, Team Leader, Peer Reviewer
2.1-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception Verify land use classification, check valuation history, confirm ownership structure, cross-check with Duties/Titles data Valuation Services data doesn't match council data. Investigator, Team Leader
2.1-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception Cross-check VWA vs PTX wages, analyse STP payroll totals, identify grouping relationships, review contractor reclassification risk Grouping is a nightmare — there's no single source of truth. Investigator, Team Leader
2.1-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception Verify occupancy using utilities data, cross-check identity documents, validate builder registration, check for multiple grants We rely on utilities data but it's slow and inconsistent. Investigator, Team Leader
2.1-Duties ◇ Duties Exception Verify property transfer against Titles Office, check valuation for undervaluation, identify foreign purchaser surcharge, review related-party docs Duties Online data is good, but conveyancer uploads are inconsistent. Investigator, Team Leader
2.2 Assess Risk & Determine Priority Determine the level of risk, complexity, and urgency to decide whether the case should proceed, be paused, or be redirected Risk is subjective — everyone does it differently. No consistent risk framework leads to inconsistent prioritisation. Investigator, Team Leader, Executive Reviewer
2.3 Proceed / Redirect / Close (DECISION GATE) Based on risk and available information, decide whether the case should proceed to planning, be redirected, or be closed We don't close things early enough — they drag on. Lack of early decision criteria leads to unnecessary progression of low-value cases. Investigator, Team Leader, Executive Reviewer
VALUE STAGE 3: INVESTIGATION PLANNING
3.1 Define Scope & Objectives Define what the investigation will examine, what evidence is required, and what outcomes are possible We jump straight into work without a plan. Lack of structured planning leads to inefficient evidence gathering and scope creep. Investigator, Team Leader, Legal / Policy Advisor
3.1-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception Determine land use classification, assess exemption eligibility, identify trust structures/beneficiaries, determine entity grouping Trusts make everything harder. Investigator, Team Leader
3.1-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception Determine grouping across entities, assess contractor vs employee classification, verify interstate allocation, identify exempt wages Contractor rules are interpreted differently by everyone. Investigator, Team Leader
3.1-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception Confirm genuine occupancy timeframe, validate first-home buyer eligibility, assess builder legitimacy, identify straw-buyer arrangements Occupancy is the hardest thing to prove. Investigator, Team Leader
3.1-Duties ◇ Duties Exception Determine correct dutiable value, assess related-party transfer legitimacy, determine foreign surcharge applicability, assess landholder duty Related-party transfers are always messy. Investigator, Team Leader
3.2 Develop Investigation Plan Create a structured plan outlining tasks, evidence sources, timelines, and dependencies Plans live in our heads — nothing is written down. Lack of documented plans reduces transparency and makes handovers difficult. Investigator, Team Leader, Legal / Policy Advisor
3.3 Obtain Approvals Seek approval for investigation scope, planned actions, or use of specific powers Approvals take forever — everything is via email. Manual approval processes create delays and lack auditability. Team Leader, Executive Reviewer, Legal / Policy Advisor
VALUE STAGE 4: EVIDENCE & ANALYSIS
4.1 Gather Evidence Collect evidence from internal systems, external agencies, customers, and third parties We chase the same documents multiple times. Lack of centralised evidence tracking leads to duplication and delays. Investigator, Team Leader, Third-Party Data Provider
4.1-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception Collect council rates notices, valuation reports, land use declarations, site photos, trust deeds, Titles Office extracts We have to ask the customer for documents council already has. Investigator, Team Leader
4.1-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception Collect payroll reports (weekly/fortnightly/monthly), STP submissions, VWA declarations, contractor agreements, timesheets, interstate breakdowns Employers give us payroll data in 20 different formats. Investigator, Team Leader
4.1-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception Collect utilities data, lease agreements, bank statements, builder contracts, occupancy declarations, driver licence history, council records Applicants give us doctored documents — we have to verify everything. Investigator, Team Leader
4.1-Duties ◇ Duties Exception Collect contract of sale, statement of adjustments, valuation reports, Titles extracts, trust/corporate docs, foreign declarations, bank statements We get 200-page contracts with no index — impossible to find anything. Investigator, Team Leader
4.2 Analyse Evidence & Identify Gaps Review collected evidence, identify inconsistencies, and determine what additional information is required We don't know what we already have — everything is buried in CM9. Poor document organisation makes evidence hard to locate. Investigator, Team Leader, Peer Reviewer
4.3 Request Additional Information Send formal or informal requests for additional information to customers or external agencies We send letters manually — no templates, no automation. Manual correspondence processes increase cycle time and introduce inconsistency. Investigator, Team Leader, Third-Party Data Provider
4.4 Conduct Interviews or Site Visits Conduct interviews, site visits, or inspections to gather further evidence or validate claims We don't have a standard way to record interviews. Lack of structured templates leads to inconsistent evidence capture. Investigator, Team Leader, Legal / Policy Advisor
4.5 Form Preliminary View Based on evidence gathered, form a preliminary view of liability, compliance, or risk Everyone interprets evidence differently. Lack of standardised assessment criteria leads to inconsistent outcomes. Investigator, Team Leader, Peer Reviewer
VALUE STAGE 5: ENFORCEMENT & RESOLUTION
5.1 Draft Findings & Notices Prepare draft findings, notices, or assessments based on the preliminary view Templates are inconsistent — everyone writes them differently. Lack of standardised templates leads to inconsistent communication and legal risk. Investigator, Team Leader, Processor, Legal / Policy Advisor
5.1-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception Land Tax Assessment Notice, Vacant Residential Land Tax Notice, Land Use Adjustment Notice, Trust Surcharge Assessment LT notices are the most complex — too many variations. Investigator, Team Leader
5.1-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception Payroll Tax Assessment, Grouping Determination Notice, Contractor Reclassification Notice, Interstate Payroll Adjustment Notice Grouping notices are the most contested — employers always push back. Investigator, Team Leader
5.1-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception FHOG Reversal Notice, FHOG Recovery Notice, FHOG Eligibility Determination, Occupancy Breach Notice FHOG reversals get messy — people get emotional. Investigator, Team Leader
5.1-Duties ◇ Duties Exception Duties Assessment Notice, Foreign Purchaser Surcharge Assessment, Landholder Duty Assessment, Off-the-Plan Concession Adjustment Notice Foreign purchaser surcharge is the most contested part. Investigator, Team Leader
5.2 Review & Approve Findings Review draft findings and notices for accuracy, consistency, and legal compliance Reviews take too long — everything is manual. Manual review processes create bottlenecks and inconsistent quality assurance. Team Leader, Executive Reviewer, Legal / Policy Advisor
5.3 Issue Notices / Assessments Send formal notices or assessments to the customer and record them in the case file We manually upload everything — nothing is automated. Manual issuance and filing processes increase administrative burden and risk of error. Investigator, Team Leader, Processor
5.4 Objections Received? (DECISION GATE) Review customer objections or responses and determine whether findings need to be updated or case should close Objections go back and forth forever. No structured objection-handling workflow leads to delays and inconsistent outcomes. Investigator, Team Leader, Legal / Policy Advisor
5.4-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception Disputes: incorrect land use, incorrect valuation, incorrect trust surcharge, incorrect exemption removal Valuation objections take forever — we depend on another team. Investigator, Team Leader
5.4-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception Disputes: grouping determination, contractor classification, wage totals (STP vs employer), interstate allocation, exempt wages Objections turn into mini-audits — we basically redo the whole case. Investigator, Team Leader
5.4-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception Disputes: occupancy evidence, claims of temporary absence, builder contract validity, identity verification, administrative error claims Objections often turn into emotional appeals rather than factual disputes. Investigator, Team Leader
5.4-Duties ◇ Duties Exception Disputes: dutiable value, foreign purchaser surcharge, related-party valuation, landholder thresholds, off-the-plan concession eligibility Duties objections are technical — they take ages to resolve. Investigator, Team Leader
5.5 Finalise Case & Close Complete final documentation, update systems, and formally close the case Closing a case takes longer than the investigation. Excessive administrative steps and duplicate filing slow down case closure. Investigator, Team Leader, Processor
VALUE STAGE 6: LEARNING & IMPROVEMENT
6.1 Capture Learnings & Insights Document key learnings, systemic issues, and opportunities for improvement We never capture learnings — everything is in people's heads. Lack of structured learning capture prevents continuous improvement. Investigator, Team Leader, Executive Reviewer
6.2 Share Insights with Other Teams Share insights with other teams to improve processes, risk models, and compliance strategies Insights never go anywhere — no one sees them. No structured mechanism for sharing insights limits organisational learning. Investigator, Team Leader, Executive Reviewer, Legal / Policy Advisor
