Context: Change of Customer Details Process AUTHORITY & SCOPE

Overview

This procedure covers processing customer requests to change postal address, name, contact information, authorised representatives, or minor property details for Land Tax purposes.

```

Request Channels

  • SmartForm (GEN-03) - Structured web/email form submission
  • My Land Tax - Online self-service portal (auto-authenticated)
  • Written correspondence - Letter or email
  • Phone calls - Verbal requests requiring documentation

Key Control Points

  • Authority: Verify requester is authorised before processing
  • Scope: Confirm request is within COCD scope
  • System Updates: Apply changes to correct e-Sys fields
  • Derivatives: Update related customer records (Trusts, JO, Groups)
Time Standard: 7.5 minutes per item | System of Record: Lotus Notes Policies and Procedures Database
Input data: Customer request, submission channel, customer structure, requester identity
Processing responsibility: Staff (authority verification, details update), e-Sys (customer records)
Decision: Is request in scope? Is requester authorised? What updates needed?
Output: Updated customer details, completed COCD action item, derivative updates if required
```

```
Address Format Codes - System Requirements

  • RSD (Residential Street Address): Standard street addresses (123 Main St, Unit 5/100 High St). Most common. Required for Australia Post validation.
  • PO Box (Post Office Box): Locked Bag, Private Bag numbers. Used for business mail receiving points.
  • C/O (Care Of): Mail addressed to person at another address (e.g., "C/O John Smith, 10 Elm Ave"). Use when correspondence goes to third party at their address.
  • Other (Special Format): Non-standard addresses, rural properties with descriptions, or addresses that don't fit standard formats.

Usage Type Selection - What Goes Where

Postal Only: Customer receives Land Tax correspondence at this address, but doesn't live there.

Example: Small investor uses accountant's office (PO Box 123) to receive tax correspondence, lives in another state.

Residential Only: This is where customer lives, but Land Tax correspondence goes somewhere else.

Example: Tenant in rental property (residential address known to us), but correspondence goes to landlord/agent.

Both: Same address for Land Tax correspondence AND residential location. Most common for owner-occupiers.

Example: Customer lives at 123 Main St and wants all Land Tax mail there.

Delivery Indicator - Special Handling

  • Standard: Normal mail, no special handling. Default for most customers.
  • Urgent: Use if customer specifically requests priority handling. Flags assessments for express mail.
  • Confidential: Use for sensitive matters (disputes, legal proceedings). Ensures mail handled securely.
  • Special: Other special handling. Document in notes what special handling means.

Customer Type Address Examples

Individual: Lives at 42 High St, Melbourne. Format=RSD, Usage=Both, Delivery=Standard

Trust: Trustee lives at 5 Park Rd. Derive address from Trustee. Format=RSD, Usage=Postal, Delivery=Standard

Joint Ownership: Lead Customer at 88 Maple Ave. All JOs get correspondence at this address. Format=RSD, Usage=Both, Delivery=Standard

  • Update flows to Land Taxpayer node (visible on assessment)
  • All related derivative customers updated if applicable (Trusts, JO, Groups)
```

PPR Eligibility Decision

Is address change related to Principal Place of Residence eligibility? Does customer want PPR applied/removed?
```

PPR Eligibility Criteria

PPR (Principal Place of Residence) exemption applies only if:

  • Address is customer's main residential address (where they live most of the time)
  • Customer owns property in own name (not investment property)
  • Customer must provide evidence of residency (utility bill, rates notice, COI check, DLS)
  • Address meets residential criteria (not vacant land, not commercial)

PPR Effective Date

PPR applied from 1 December of current year → affects next year's assessment

PPR Application Process

  1. Verify address qualifies for PPR (residential, customer primary residence)
  2. Confirm customer wants PPR applied
  3. Apply PPR flag from 1 December current year
  4. PPR exemption will flow to next year's assessment (following year)
  5. Document PPR application in action item notes
Procedure Detail: PPR Eligibility Criteria, Verification Sources, 1 December Effective Date Explanation & Removal Process▼

What is PPR (Principal Place of Residence)?

PPR is a land tax exemption available to owner-occupiers of residential properties. If a property qualifies for PPR, the liability is waived for that year. PPR is NOT available for investors (properties held for rental income).

Full PPR Eligibility Criteria - All Must Be Met

1. Property Type: Must be residential (house, apartment, townhouse). NOT vacant land, NOT commercial, NOT agricultural.

2. Ownership: Customer must own property (be on title). Tenants don't qualify.

3. Primary Residence: Property must be where customer lives most of the time (principal place of residence, not investment/holiday home).

4. Use: Property must not be used for business or rental. Owner-occupied only.

5. Proof of Residency: Customer must provide evidence they live there.

Residency Verification Sources (in priority order)

1. RTBA (Rental Tenancy Bond Authority): If customer previously listed as tenant at this address, they've lived there (if recent).

2. DLS (Driver License System): Address on driver license = residential address (if current).

3. COI (Compliance Operational Intelligence): Government linkage of customer to address.

4. Utility Bills: Council rates, water bill, electricity bill showing this address (must be current or very recent).

5. Customer Declaration: Customer states they live there; use if other sources unavailable (document in notes).

Why 1 December Effective Date?

Explanation: Land Tax assessments are issued on specific dates each year. To ensure PPR exemption is applied to the following financial year, it must be entered into the system by 1 December of the current year. If PPR is applied after 1 December, it doesn't affect the next year's assessment (flows to year after).

Example:

  • Today: 30 October 2024
  • Customer moves to new residential address
  • We apply PPR flag on 30 October
  • PPR affects 2024-25 assessment (issued after 1 December cutoff)

PPR Removal Process

When to Remove: If customer sells property, moves to investment property, or no longer qualifies (e.g., becomes investment property).

Process: Remove PPR flag in e-Sys, effective date is removal date. Future assessments will include full land tax liability.

Verification: Require proof of sale or change of use before removing PPR.

PPR in e-Sys System Field

Location: Property/Valuation section

Entry: Set PPR = Yes/No, with effective date (1 December or specific date)

Result: Next assessment run includes PPR exemption if flag = Yes

Input data: Address change, PPR eligibility indicators, residency evidence
Processing responsibility: Staff (eligibility assessment), e-Sys (PPR flag application)
Decision: Does address qualify for PPR? Apply or remove?
Output: PPR applied/removed effective 1 December (or specified date)
```

```
Procedure Detail: Detailed Derivative Update Processes per Structure Type with Verification Steps▼

Trust Derivative Updates - Detailed Process

Customer Structure: Trust owns property; Trustee is responsible party for Land Tax.

How Derivative Works: Trust customer details (address, contact) are linked to Trustee customer. Changes to Trustee automatically flow to Trust.

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Request is for Trust address change (e.g., trustee moved to new address)
  2. Access Trustee customer record in e-Sys
  3. Update Trustee address to new address
  4. Check Trust customer record → confirm address has auto-updated
  5. Verify derived address matches Trustee address
  6. Document in COCD action item: "Trust address derived from Trustee [date]"

Troubleshooting: If Trust address doesn't auto-derive, escalate to Grade 3 for system check.

Joint Ownership Derivative Updates - Detailed Process

Customer Structure: Multiple customers own property jointly. Lead Customer receives correspondence; change affects all JO members.

How Derivative Works: All JO members linked to Lead Customer. Correspondence goes to Lead Customer's address; all JOs get copies.

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Request is for JO address change (e.g., Lead Customer moved)
  2. Verify requester is Lead Customer (only Lead can authorize address changes)
  3. Access Lead Customer record
  4. Update Lead Customer address
  5. Check JO mailing list → confirm all JO members listed
  6. Verify correspondence will go to Lead's new address and be copied to all JOs
  7. Document in COCD action item: "JO address updated [date]. All JO members: [names]. Correspondence to Lead at [new address]"

Important: All JO members must receive correspondence. If any JO member has different address requirement, escalate (out of scope for standard COCD).

Land Tax Group Derivative Updates - Detailed Process

Customer Structure: Multiple properties grouped together for Land Tax assessment. Nominated Member receives group correspondence; changes affect entire group.

How Derivative Works: All properties in group linked to Nominated Member. Correspondence goes to Nominated Member's address.

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Request is for group address change (e.g., Nominated Member moved)
  2. Verify requester is Nominated Member (only Nom Member can authorize)
  3. Access Nominated Member customer record
  4. Update Nominated Member address
  5. Check Land Tax Group record → confirm all properties listed in group
  6. Verify correspondence will go to Nominated Member's new address for all group properties
  7. Document in COCD action item: "Land Tax Group address updated [date]. Nominated Member: [name]. Properties in group: [list]. Group correspondence to [new address]"

Important: All properties in group must have correspondence go to same address (Nominated Member). If any property needs different address, escalate.
```

In-Scope vs Out-of-Scope Criteria

IN SCOPE (8 items): Address (postal/residential), Name, Contact (phone/email), Representatives (add/amend/remove), Minor property detail

OUT OF SCOPE (7+ categories): Ownership changes, Liability disputes, PPR challenges, Valuation objections, Property subdivisions, Customer type change, Assessment year changes

Decision Rule: If request is about WHO owns, WHERE structurally, WHAT it's worth, or HOW assessed → OUT OF SCOPE. Refer to Senior Officer.
