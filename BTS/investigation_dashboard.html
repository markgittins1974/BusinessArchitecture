Key Analytical Read-out

  • Largest volume cohort: Land Tax – PPR (excl streamlined/simplified) with 3,154 cases. This is the biggest scale lever for aggregate productivity improvement.
  • Best timeliness: Trust (incl PEXA NOA Full Audit, excl Streamlined) at 19.10% outside KPI and 8.52 hours per case.
  • Worst timeliness: VRLT FY2024/25 at 55.79% outside KPI, indicating major flow or capability issues.
  • Highest effort: Payroll Tax – ATO/BAS Registered at 30.40 hours per case, making it the strongest efficiency-improvement candidate.
  • Payroll Tax pattern: Registered cases are materially worse than Unregistered on both timeliness (53.81% vs 35.45% outside KPI) and effort (30.40 vs 22.77 hours per case).
  • VRLT trend: FY2025/26 YTD timeliness is better than FY2024/25 (43.11% vs 55.79% outside KPI), but hours per case remain high (13.93 vs 13.22), suggesting process discipline may be improving faster than efficiency.
  • Stable cohorts: Duties and most Land Tax/Trust cohorts cluster around ~19%–23% outside KPI and ~8.5–11.5 hours per case, suggesting more repeatable execution patterns.

Average hours per case by cohort

Improvement Priority Framing

  • Scale lever: Land Tax PPR has the largest case volume, so small improvements can create large aggregate capacity release.
  • Efficiency lever: Payroll Tax, particularly Registered, has the highest labour cost per case and very poor timeliness.
  • Capability uplift lever: VRLT remains high-effort even where timeliness has improved, indicating a likely maturity, training or standardisation issue.
  • Benchmark candidate: Trust is the strongest large-volume performer and may provide repeatable practices worth reusing.
