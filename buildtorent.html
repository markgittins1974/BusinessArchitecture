Q1 – Is the development eligible for BTR benefits?

  • Assess the development against all nine eligibility requirements.
  • Confirm the building is new or substantially renovated and constructed for residential lease dwellings.
  • Confirm minimum 50 self-contained dwellings forming the BTR development.
  • Confirm collectively owned within a unified ownership structure.
  • Confirm managed by a single entity.
  • Confirm occupancy date on or after 1 January 2021 and before 1 January 2032 (evidenced by occupancy permit).
  • Confirm dwellings available for rent to general public under residential rental agreements (Residential Tenancies Act 1997).
  • Confirm minimum 3-year lease offered per dwelling (tenant may choose lesser period).
  • Confirm no rental restrictions except public health/safety or social affordable housing.
  • From 2026: Confirm genuine 3-year offer made. If lesser term entered, joint signed declaration required from BTR provider and renter. Declaration must be provided to Commissioner upon request.
Do not proceed if any eligibility requirement is not met.
Procedure reference: Determining Eligibility (p.4); Eligibility Requirements (p.2); Quick Reference Guide (p.3)
Input data:
  • Development details (address, building description, construction/renovation status)
  • Number of self-contained dwellings (minimum 50 threshold)
  • Ownership structure (collectively owned, unified)
  • Management entity (single entity requirement)
  • Occupancy date / occupancy permit (on or after 1 Jan 2021, before 1 Jan 2032)
  • Rental terms:
    • Available to general public
    • Residential rental agreements per Residential Tenancies Act 1997
    • Minimum 3-year lease offered
  • Rental restrictions (none permitted except health/safety or social affordable housing)
  • From 2026: genuine offer evidence, joint signed declaration if lesser term
  • Customer-submitted information and documentation per SRO website requirements
Processing responsibility:
  • SRO assessing staff — eligibility assessment
  • e‑Sys — application recording
Decision: Eligible (all nine criteria satisfied) OR Not Eligible (any criterion fails).
Output:
  • Eligibility confirmed → proceed to Q2
  • Ineligibility recorded → application not progressed, customer advised

Q2 – Does the development consist of multiple separately rated properties?

  • Identify whether properties are separately rated.
  • If multiple properties: create a BTR parcel to link all properties in the development.
  • The parcel is for record-keeping only — it has no functional effect on concessions. The concession must still be applied to each property separately (see Q3).
  • Select a lead property. The lead property has no special significance (unlike valuation parcels) — each property remains individually rateable with its own municipal valuation.
  • Load customer and all required properties to the tree. Navigate to parcel node, right-click, select "New BTR Parcel." Enter the start date (= BTR benefits start date). Press "Associate Property" and select all properties, then click "Associate" and save.
  • Properties can be added to an existing parcel without breaking it — press Edit then Associate. Properties must be owned by the same customer.
  • Properties can be added in blocks to reduce e‑Sys load when dealing with large numbers (50+).
  • The parcel screen shows total property count and parcel age per assessing year — this is how SRO monitors the 50-dwelling threshold.
Procedure reference: Creating a Parcel (p.5–8)
Input data:
  • Property rating data (separately rated or single property)
  • Ownership data (all properties must be same customer)
  • Current parcel status (existing BTR parcel or none)
  • BTR benefits start date
Processing responsibility: e‑Sys — parcel creation and property association.
Decision: Single property (no parcel required) OR Multiple separately rated properties (BTR parcel required).
Output:
  • Single property → no parcel created, proceed to Q3
  • Multiple properties → BTR parcel created, lead property nominated, all properties associated, total dwelling count and parcel age visible per assessing year
Execution constraints:
  • Parcel is record-keeping only — no functional effect on concessions
  • Properties can be appended without breaking the parcel (Edit → Associate)
  • Properties can be added in blocks to manage e‑Sys load
  • Same-customer ownership required for all properties in parcel

Q3 – Is the BTR concession to be applied to each eligible property?

  • Apply the BTR concession to each eligible property individually via Property Exemptions screen MC580 (right-click → new exemption).
  • For bulk application: use the "bulk functions" button on MC580 — select all properties, maximum 100 per batch.
  • Warning: Applying the concession to the lead property does NOT apply it to the entire parcel. Each property must be done individually or via bulk.
  • Where only part of the land is used for eligible BTR development: update the eligible proportion in MC570 under Property Details. Section 22 of the Land Tax Act 2005 applies to determine taxable value of each part.
  • Applying a partial or full BTR concession automatically exempts the property from the Absentee Owner Surcharge (AOS).
Applying to the lead property does NOT cascade to the parcel. Each property must be done individually or via bulk.
Cross-procedure link: BTR concession triggers AOS exemption — see AOS Procedure (DOC/18/509543)
Procedure reference: Applying Build to Rent Concession (p.9–10)
Input data:
  • Eligible property list (from Q1 and Q2)
  • Land usage proportions (full BTR use or partial)
  • e‑Sys screens: MC580 (Property Exemptions), MC570 (Property Details — partial proportion)
Processing responsibility:
  • e‑Sys — concession application (individual via MC580 or bulk)
  • Staff — partial proportion entry in MC570
Decision: Full concession (entire land used for BTR) OR Partial concession (Section 22 apportionment applies).
Output:
  • BTR concession applied to each property (individually or bulk, max 100 per batch)
  • Proportionate concession applied where partial land eligibility (set in MC570)
  • AOS exemption automatically applied to each concession property
Execution constraints:
  • Lead property concession does NOT cascade to parcel
  • Bulk application limit: 100 properties per batch
  • Partial proportion set in MC570 under Property Details tab

Q4 – Have post-application administrative steps been completed?

  • Restrict automatic Property Clearance Certificate generation: navigate to Land Taxpayer node, tick "Prevent PIA Auto Gen", add comment noting BTR customer.
  • Record application and outcome in the BTR applications spreadsheet at H:\LandTax\Common\Land_tax\LTX Assessing\BTR.
  • Send correspondence to customer advising of outcome. Correspondence must cover BTR obligations and Special Tax implications (cessation within 15 years triggers BTR special land tax).
  • Templates are in the Assisted Digital One Note and the LAT Correspondence Document.
Interim measures: PIA auto-gen prevention and spreadsheet recording are both interim measures pending system updates. These steps may be replaced by system-native controls in future.
Procedure reference: Last steps and customer communication (p.11)
Input data:
  • Approved concession records (from Q3)
  • Customer communication requirements (Special Tax obligation disclosure)
Processing responsibility:
  • e‑Sys — PIA auto-gen restriction (Land Taxpayer node)
  • Shared H drive — BTR spreadsheet (H:\LandTax\Common\Land_tax\LTX Assessing\BTR)
  • Assisted Digital / LAT Correspondence Document — correspondence templates
Decision: None — administrative closure.
Output:
  • PIA auto-generation restricted with BTR comment
  • BTR applications spreadsheet updated
  • Customer correspondence issued (outcome, obligations, Special Tax implications)
Interim flags: PIA restriction and spreadsheet recording are both interim measures. Future-state design should anticipate these being replaced by system-native controls.

Procedure Summary

  • Defines the process for determining eligibility for BTR land tax benefits and applying those benefits to qualifying developments.
  • Covers eligibility assessment against nine legislated criteria, parcel creation for multi-property developments, concession application (individual and bulk), and post-decision administration including interim reporting controls.
  • BTR benefits: 50% reduction on taxable land value for up to 30 years, plus AOS exemption. Total entitlement up to 60 years subject to 15 continuous years of eligibility. Cessation within 15 years triggers BTR special land tax.

Eligibility Criteria Summary (9 requirements)

  1. New or substantially renovated building on land
  2. Constructed/renovated for residential lease dwellings
  3. Minimum 50 self-contained dwellings
  4. Collectively owned, unified ownership structure
  5. Single management entity
  6. Occupancy date: on or after 1 Jan 2021, before 1 Jan 2032
  7. Available for rent to general public (Residential Tenancies Act 1997)
  8. Minimum 3-year lease offered per dwelling
  9. No rental restrictions (except health/safety or social affordable housing)

From 2026: Genuine 3-year offer required. Joint signed declaration if lesser term. Commissioner may disregard uninhabitable periods.

Structural Limitations and Ambiguities

Eligibility determination: Nine criteria are narrative-described rather than expressed as discrete testable conditions. Heavy reliance on supporting documentation assessment.
Parcel management: Iterative property addition (append-only model) is flexible but difficult to express as a single decision. No defined parcel size limit.
Interim workarounds: PIA auto-gen prevention and spreadsheet recording are flagged as interim measures with no timeline or trigger for replacement.
Cross-procedure dependency: BTR concession automatically triggers AOS exemption. The AOS procedure (DOC/18/509543) does not reference BTR as a source of AOS exemption.
2026 legislative amendments: New conditions (genuine offer, joint declaration) apply from 1 January 2026 but procedure revision date is not confirmed.
