About This Diagram

This diagram documents the steps of buying, selling and owning property in Victoria. It provides a holistic, customer-focused view which includes the roles of SRO and key systems within this journey.

Based on 30 hour-long interviews conducted in June 2020.

Actions

Prompt for people to buy or sell — they commence their journey.

Discussions with trusted family and friends.

Seeking advice from trusted professional sources.

Discussions with experienced family and friends.

Seeking advice from professionals (accountants, conveyancers, lawyers, agents, financiers).

Web research of trusted sites. Attend auctions.

Buying: Secure finance, find a property, make an offer.

Selling: Find an agent, list property, accept an offer.

Find a conveyancer or property solicitor.

Section 32 creation / review.

Sign DOL form.

Pay liabilities (including LTD, GAIC if applicable).

Pay arising ownership costs (Land Tax, Absentee Owner Surcharge, VRLT, Fire Services Property Levy, GAIC if applicable).
Mindset

Buying mindset:

  • Want to stop paying rent — "dead money"
  • Ready to get into the market
  • Ready to size up (changing family needs)
  • Need a smaller home, downsizing
  • Ready to invest

Selling mindset:

  • Want to downsize or upsize
  • Changing needs / separation
  • Investment property costing too much
  • Adjusting investment strategy

Often a sense of urgency — want to buy or sell quickly.

Uncertainty for people new to buying/selling.

"Not many people know about this stuff. If you have not gone through it you don't know."
— P10 Buyer

New buyers/sellers seek lots of information: steps involved, borrowing capacity, which professionals to use, costs including taxes, government support.

1 sro.vic.gov.au reported as a reliable source of truth.

Repeat buyers/sellers more confident, rely on existing professional relationships.

Assumptions about property values and costs are tested.

First time buyers: Pre-approval time consuming; making an offer overwhelming; budget often under grant thresholds; additional costs must be factored in; learning along the way.

Experienced buyers: Less emotional, look for value, may enquire about unlisted properties.

Sellers: Experience determined by selling within timeframe at acceptable price.

Desire for settlement on time without issue — can be very stressful.

Good conveyancer/solicitor seen as important: trusted information source, technical knowledge, S32 support, smooth settlement.

Buyers/sellers rely on conveyancer/solicitor for guidance.

Land Transfer Duty: Most understand LTD is payable but see it as a burden. FHO/PPR buyers see it as required cost. Repeat buyers factor into calculations.

2 LTD calculators widely available; SRO's described as straightforward.

3 Some seek SRO confirmation — positive experiences.

DOL: 4 Generally straightforward. 5 SRO call centre always helpful.

Investment property owners often unaware of land tax until first assessment. Confusion and anxiety common.

"No one knows about land tax until they get a bill."
— P1 SRO Contact Centre

Better understood by those with multiple properties. Some representatives try to make clients aware, but not common.
Needs
  • Assurance about decisions
  • Understanding of broader financial implications
  • Validation from trusted sources
  • Understand steps of buying/selling
  • Understand costs (LTD, fees, etc.)
  • Understand eligibility for government support (FHOG, concessions)
  • Clear information about offer types
  • Obligations in negotiation (auction/private sale)
  • Cost adjustment based on offer price

Buyers: Fully understand property detail and underlying issues.

Both: Conveyancing handled without unnecessary effort. Settlement on time, no unforeseen cost.

Settlement without issues, on time.

Budget for ownership costs with no surprises.
Pain Points

Buyer: Unaware of what to do, who to speak to, what order (first timers). Not aware of investment property costs vs PPR.

1 Not aware which taxes are State or Federal.

Seller: Selling in poor market. Land tax increases. Unsure of fees.

Steps, costs and subsidies still unclear despite research.

Costs discovered only after first experience (rates, water, capital gains, PEXA fees, land tax).

2 Government explanations too detailed — no snapshot with drill-down.

Easier to call than search online.

Difficulty finding property within budget. Paying more than anticipated increases costs.

3 Limited understanding of LTD exemptions/concessions.

FHOs ineligible for FHOG due to higher price. Confusion over conditional vs unconditional offers.

Unexpected costs (e.g. unpaid land tax). DOL/PEXA integration issues delay settlement.

4 Unsigning/resigning — clients resign same form five times.

Large developments:

  • 5 DOL cumbersome for bulk — filtering 50+ forms, 50 emails to clients
  • DOL/PEXA language misalignment
  • No DOL-PEXA integration

Brokers: Want PEXA visibility without doing work in it.

Buyers seek extra SRO confirmation on LTD calculation.

6 Surprise at first land tax bill.

"I'm a chartered accountant. I studied tax. But I didn't find out about land tax until I got the letter."
— P19 Repeat Buyer

Poor understanding of when payable and how calculated. $250k threshold unchanged while prices rose. Selling with land tax debt causes confusion.

  • Support buyers/sellers to access resources and understand steps

  • New technologies (chatbots with real person)
