Q0 - Is it the right time to calculate and publish the new PCC fee?NEW

✓ Check the financial year calendar. PCC fee updates occur annually before 1 July (start of financial year).

⏰ CRITICAL TIMING: Fee unit is published March-May (typically April-May). You must start checking in late March.

  • Current Month: What month are we in? (see Timeline in Summary view for exact windows)
  • Financial Year Boundary: Is it between late March and 30 June? (Typical update window)
  • Budget Timing: Note that fee unit determination is aligned with annual Budget release (typically April-May)
  • Decision: Is it the appropriate time to begin fee unit monitoring and calculation?
TIMING DECISION: Is it March-June? YES→Continue to Q1. NO→Wait for appropriate window next financial year.
Why timing matters: Fee unit is published unpredictably within March-May window. You must monitor continuously during this period. Publishing new fee before 1 July is non-negotiable deadline.
Input data: Current calendar date, financial year information
Processing responsibility: KMB staff (monitoring), DTF/Treasury (fee unit determination)
Decision: Is it March-June (update window)? (YES/NO)
Output: Proceed to Q1 if YES; wait if NO

Q1 - Has the annual fee unit been published by the Treasurer?IMPROVED

📋 SEQUENCE REQUIREMENT: Cannot calculate PCC fee without current fee unit. This is the gate-keeping step.

  1. Check DTF Website: Visit https://www.dtf.vic.gov.au/ and search for "fee unit" or "Monetary Units Act"
  2. Check Victorian Government Gazette: Visit https://www.gazettes.vic.gov.au/ and search for "fee unit" determination
  3. Document the Source: Note the publication date and gazette reference. You will need this for verification (Q2).
  4. Extract Fee Unit Value: The determination will show the annual fee unit amount (e.g., "$187.50" for FY2024-25)
  5. Verify Publication Date: Confirm publication occurred (should be gazetted before you begin Q2)
  6. DECISION POINT - Fee Unit Status:

    What is the status?

    • IF Published: Note exact fee unit value. Proceed to Q2.
    • IF Pending (not yet published): Document date you checked. Retry check next business day. Note: Typically publishes by end of May.
    • IF Delayed beyond 31 May: Escalate to Knowledge Management Branch Manager. May impact 1 July deadline.
  7. Document Check: Record in Q1 verification log: DTF check date, Gazette check date, fee unit value, publication reference
Do NOT proceed with calculation until fee unit is published. Do NOT assume value from previous year. Verify with current year determination only.
Input data: DTF website, Victorian Government Gazette, current date
Processing responsibility: KMB staff (verification), DTF/Treasury (publication)
Decision: Fee unit published and documented? (YES/NO)
Output: Confirmed fee unit value and publication reference; proceed to Q2; or defer if not published

Q2 - Calculate the property clearance certificate feeIMPROVED

✓ Now that you have the fee unit, use it to calculate the PCC fee using the statutory formula.

  1. Recall the Formula: PCC Fee = 1.16 × Annual Fee Unit Value (from Q1)
  2. Perform Calculation: Multiply 1.16 × the fee unit value from Q1
    Example: If fee unit = $187.50, then PCC fee = 1.16 × $187.50 = $217.50
  3. ROUNDING DECISION (Critical - GAP 3 Addressed):

    Apply Section 7(3) MUA2004 Rounding Rule:

    Round the calculated amount to the nearest 10 cents:

    • Amounts ending in $X.X0 – $X.X4: Round DOWN to $X.X0
    • Amounts ending in $X.X5 – $X.X9: Round UP to $(X+0.10)

    Examples: $217.37 → $217.40 | $217.34 → $217.30 | $217.45 → $217.50 | $217.54 → $217.50

  4. Round Your Result: Apply the rounding rule above. Document original and rounded amounts.
  5. Verification Step (GAP 9 Addressed): Before proceeding, verify:
    • Formula applied correctly (1.16 × fee unit)
    • Rounding applied to nearest 10 cents
    • Result is reasonable (typically between $180-$230)
    • Both original and rounded amounts documented
  6. Document Result: Record: Calculation date, Fee unit value, Calculation (1.16 × $X), Original amount, Rounded amount, Rounding rule applied
  7. Decision: Has the PCC fee been calculated correctly and verified?
CRITICAL: Rounding must follow Section 7(3) MUA2004 rule exactly. Incorrect rounding will produce wrong fee and public impact.
Input data: Fee unit value from Q1, statutory multiplier (1.16), rounding rule (Section 7(3) MUA2004)
Processing responsibility: KMB staff (calculation and verification)
Decision: Calculation correct, rounding applied, verification complete? (YES/NO)
Output: Finalised PCC fee amount (rounded); proceed to Q3 system updates
Procedure Detail: Rounding Methodology and Additional Examples▼

Rounding Rule (Section 7(3) Monetary Units Act 2004)

The amount of a fee calculated may be rounded to the nearest 10 cents.

Standard Rounding Definition: "Nearest 10 cents" means standard commercial rounding to the nearest $0.10.

Rounding Examples (Comprehensive)

Original Amount Last Two Digits Rounding Rule Rounded Amount
$217.31 .31 Between .00-.04 → DOWN $217.30
$217.34 .34 Between .00-.04 → DOWN $217.30
$217.35 .35 Between .05-.09 → UP $217.40
$217.49 .49 Between .05-.09 → UP $217.50
$217.50 .50 Exactly .50 → UP $217.50
$217.69 .69 Between .05-.09 → UP $217.70

Q3 - Update all systems with the new PCC feeIMPROVED

📋 SEQUENCE & DEADLINE: Multiple systems must be updated by 1 June to meet 1 July effective date. Follow this sequence.

  1. STEP 1 - Notify Land Revenue Branch (Immediate):
    • Send email to: S105.Escalation@sro.vic.gov.au
    • Subject: "New PCC Fee - [FY2024-25] - $217.50"
    • Content: Fee amount, calculation date, fee unit value, effective date (1 July)
    • Purpose: Triggers their systems to be prepared for update
    • Timing: Within 1 business day of calculation completion
  2. STEP 2 - Update TaxNet (By 1 June):
    • Contact: TaxNet Support or Land Revenue Branch
    • Update location: PCC fee configuration in TaxNet
    • What to update: Set new fee amount effective 1 July 2024
    • Dual-display requirement: Both old AND new fees shown until 1 July (see GAP 5 detail)
    • Example format: "PCC fee is $218.50 ($219.20 from 1 July 2024)"
  3. STEP 3 - Update SRO Website (By 1 June):
    • https://www.sro.vic.gov.au/land-tax/property-clearance-certificates
    • https://www.sro.vic.gov.au/buying-property
    • https://www.sro.vic.gov.au/land-tax/apply-property-clearance-certificate
    • SharePoint - TaxNet-LRA: Property-clearance-certificates-overview-for-CSB-LRB.aspx
    • SharePoint - TaxNet-KMB: PCC-essentials.aspx
  4. DUAL-FEE DISPLAY DECISION (Critical - GAP 5 Addressed):

    Date Range: 1 June to 30 June (30 days)

    What to display on public-facing pages:

    • OLD fee: Current fee (e.g., $218.50)
    • NEW fee: From 1 July (e.g., $219.20)
    • Format example: "The property clearance certificate fee is $218.50. From 1 July 2024, the fee will be $219.20."

    After 1 July: Display only new fee amount

  5. STEP 4 - Communicate to Staff (By 1 June):
    • Publish in "In The Know" (internal news)
    • Add TaxNet news item on PCC page
    • Send staff email with: Old fee, new fee, effective date, where to find updated info
    • Timing: Early-to-mid June (latest by 30 June)
  6. Verify All Updates (QA Step): Before 1 July:
    • TaxNet displays correct new fee
    • Website pages updated (all 5 links above)
    • Dual-fee display showing old + new (until 1 July)
    • Staff notified via In The Know + email
    • Land Revenue Branch confirmed receipt
  7. Document Completion: Record update completion date and confirmation from each system
  8. Decision: Have all systems been updated by 1 July with correct fees and dual-display messaging?
CRITICAL DEADLINE: All updates must be complete by 1 July (effective date). Incomplete updates may cause incorrect fee charges to public.
Input data: Finalised PCC fee from Q2, effective date (1 July), old fee amount for dual-display
Processing responsibility: KMB (coordination), Land Revenue Branch (TaxNet), Web team (SRO website), Communications (staff notification)
Decision: All systems updated and verified by 1 July deadline? (YES/NO)
Output: Public and internal systems showing correct fees; staff informed; process complete

Q4 - Verify compliance with all legislative referencesNEW

✓ Final verification step: Confirm calculation and publication complies with all legislation.

  • Land Tax Regulations 2015, r7: Verify PCC fee is exactly 1.16 fee units (no deviations)
  • Monetary Units Act 2004, s6: Verify fee unit used is from current Treasurer determination (not previous year)
  • Monetary Units Act 2004, s7(3): Verify fee has been rounded to nearest 10 cents per statute
  • Taxation Administration Act 1997: Verify PCC is configured for all applicable property types (land tax, VRLT, WGT)
  • State Taxation Acts Amendment Act 2020: Verify you're using current "Property Clearance Certificate" name (not legacy s105/clearance names)
Why verification matters: Incorrect legislative compliance could result in incorrect fee charges, audit findings, or public complaints.
Input data: Finalised fee, legislative references, current regulations
Processing responsibility: KMB staff (verification)
Decision: All legislative requirements met? (YES/NO)
Output: Compliance verified or issues escalated; process complete

Timeline and Calendar - Annual PCC Fee Update

LATE MARCH – Start Monitoring

Treasury typically releases fee unit determination between late March and May, aligned with Budget release. Begin checking DTF website and Victorian Government Gazette daily.

```

APRIL-MAY – Typical Publication Window

Most commonly, fee unit is gazetted in April or May (occasionally into early June). Note exact publication date and gazette reference when found.

DATE FEE UNIT PUBLISHED – Calculate Fee

Within 1 business day of finding fee unit, calculate PCC fee using formula: 1.16 × fee unit value, then round to nearest 10 cents. Document calculation.

DATE OF CALCULATION + 1 DAY – Notify Land Revenue

Email S105.Escalation@sro.vic.gov.au with new fee amount and calculation details. This triggers their system preparation.

BY 1 JUNE – Update All Systems

Complete all updates: TaxNet, website (5 URLs), staff communications. Display dual fees (old and new) until 1 July.

EARLY-TO-MID JUNE – Staff Communication

Publish in In The Know, TaxNet news, and email. Inform staff of fee change, old amount, new amount, effective date.

BY 30 JUNE – Final Verification

Verify all systems display correct fees and dual-display messaging. Confirm Land Revenue Branch ready. Check legislative compliance.

1 JULY – New Fee Effective

New PCC fee becomes official. Public and internal systems must show new fee only (dual-fee display ends). Process complete.

```

Definitions and Acronyms

  • PCC - Property Clearance Certificate (confirms land tax/VRLT/WGT debt on property)
  • Fee Unit - Monetary amount determined annually by Victorian Treasurer under Monetary Units Act 2004
  • KMB - Knowledge Management Branch (Strategic Development and Customer Engagement)
  • DTF - Department of Treasury and Finance (publishes fee unit determinations)
  • MUA2004 - Monetary Units Act 2004 (defines fee unit and rounding rules)
  • LTA - Land Tax Act 2005 (sets PCC fee at 1.16 fee units, r7 Land Tax Regulations 2015)
  • TAA - Taxation Administration Act 1997 (PCC provisions)
  • TaxNet - SRO's internal system for managing land tax and PCC administration
  • SRO - State Revenue Office (Victoria)
  • Rounding - Section 7(3) MUA2004: amount may be rounded to nearest 10 cents
  • Dual-Fee Display - Showing both old and new fees during 30-day transition period (1-30 June)
  • Effective Date - 1 July (when new fee becomes official)
