Assessing Quality - Procedure Overview

Purpose

Detail the process Land Assessing Team (LAT) staff should undertake when quality checking tasks completed by colleagues to ensure consistent, equitable results for taxpayers and maximised front-end compliance.

Background

  • Who performs QA: Grade 3 and Grade 4 LAT staff only
  • What's checked: All refund items >$10,000 PLUS random sample of 5 items per staff member per month
  • Special threshold: Items >$99,999 must be checked by Grade 4 Branch Technical Officer (BTO)
  • Pass/Fail threshold: 95% or above = PASS; 94% or below = FAIL
  • Monthly KPI: Minimum acceptable accuracy is 95%

Key Process Steps (Q1-Q5)

  1. Q1 (Monthly): Quality Checkers submit refund QA >$10,000 to Quality Check Form
  2. Q2 (1st Business Day): Team Leaders extract MC953 and VT920 reports, select random sample
  3. Q3 (First Week): Quality Checkers assess items against Quality Checklist, record results
  4. Q4 (By Day 5 Next Month): Team Leaders confirm results, calculate team metrics
  5. Q5 (Monthly 1:1): Team Leaders provide feedback and results to staff members
Authority: Division: Land Revenue Services | Branch: Land Revenue Administration | Revised: 4 December 2025 | Effective: 1 January 2024
Input: Completed action items (refunds, amendments), MC953/VT920 reports, random sample
Process: Q1-Q5: Submit → Select → Check → Record → Feedback
Decision: Does item meet 95%+ threshold? Is sample valid? Are results captured correctly?
Output: QA results (PASS/FAIL), feedback provided, monthly team KPI calculated

Q1 - Submit Refund QA (Items >$10,000)

Monthly Submission Timeframe

Due: 1st to last business day of each month

Who: Quality Checker (Grade 3 or 4)

Process Steps

  1. Complete QA on all action items resulting in refunds >$10,000 during the month
  2. For each item, assess against Quality Checklist (see Q3 and Summary section)
  3. Insert details into Quality Check Form (Microsoft Form link provided in e-Sys)
  4. For each item, record:
    • Staff member name
    • QA reason: "Refund"
    • Customer number
    • Action Item (A/I) type (e.g., PPR, PPL, Land Ownership, Refund)
    • Quality assessment (YES/NO for each checklist item)
    • QA comments
  5. Submit form (option to request email receipt for staff feedback)
Note: Refund approvals under $10,000 do NOT require QA submission (these involve confirmation of EFT details only).
Procedure Detail: Quality Check Form▼

Form Location and Access

Quality Check Form is a Microsoft Form accessible via e-Sys link or direct URL.

Form Fields

  • Staff member name (dropdown)
  • QA reason (Refund or Random)
  • Customer number
  • Action Item (A/I) type
  • Quality assessment checkpoints (YES/NO)
  • QA comments (text field)

Form Submission Options

  • Standard: Submit form, results go to secure spreadsheet
  • Receipt: Request "Receipt of response" to email staff member immediately
  • FAIL results: PDF receipt should be provided to staff, CC Team Leader

Data Storage

Results stored in secure, password-protected Excel spreadsheet. Access restricted to LAT Team Leaders and nominated coaches/mentors.

Input: Completed action items (refund amount >$10,000), quality assessment results
Process: Quality Checker evaluates item against checklist, submits via Form
Decision: Item meets 95%+ standard (PASS) or not (FAIL)
Output: QA result recorded in Form and secure spreadsheet

Q2 - Select Random Sample (MC953 & VT920)

Monthly Timing

Due: First business day of each month

Who: Team Leader

Process Steps

  1. Obtain MC953 Report:
    • Navigate: H:\LandTax\Common\Land_tax\Esys\Reports\*month*
    • File name: esysprod_mc953m_Part_A_LTX_[date].csv
    • IMPORTANT: Report for November appears in December folder
    • Contains: All LTX action items completed during month
  2. Obtain VT920 Report:
    • File name: esysprod_VT920A_Completed_VLT_WorkItemsReport_[date].csv
    • Weekly report: consolidate into monthly summary
    • Contains: All VRLT work items completed
  3. Extract Random Sample:
    • For each staff member (Grade 2, 3, 4): randomly select 5 items
    • Exclude items already checked as "Refund" (>$10,000)
    • Sample represents diverse work types completed that month
  4. Save to Quality Assurance:
    • Save sample list to Quality Assurance SharePoint folder
    • Email quality checkers advising data available for QA
CRITICAL: Ensure items being checked are completed in the CORRECT MONTH. Items completed in February must be checked for February; don't carry January items forward.
Procedure Detail: Report Locations and Naming▼

MC953 Report Details

What it contains: All LTX action items completed in the month

Location: H:\LandTax\Common\Land_tax\Esys\Reports\[MONTH FOLDER]

File naming: esysprod_mc953m_Part_A_LTX_[timestamp].csv

Generated: Last calendar day of month

IMPORTANT NOTE: The November report will appear in the December folder (not November folder). Always check the following month's folder if not found.

VT920A Report Details

What it contains: All VRLT (Vacant Residential Land Tax) work items

Frequency: Weekly report (consolidated monthly)

File naming: esysprod_VT920A_Completed_VLT_WorkItemsReport_[timestamp].csv

Report Consolidation

Multiple weekly VT920A reports must be consolidated into one monthly summary for sampling purposes.

Input: MC953 and VT920A reports for the month
Process: Team Leader extracts reports, selects 5 random items per staff member
Decision: Is sample statistically valid? Are items from correct month?
Output: Random sample list saved, quality checkers notified

Q3 - Assess Quality Against Checklist

Assessment Timing

Due: First week of month (completed random sample checks)

Who: Quality Checker (Grade 3 or 4)

Quality Assessment Process

  1. Work through list of random items provided by Team Leader
  2. For each item, assess against Quality Checklist (see Summary section for full checklist)
  3. Quality Checklist has 9 components with weightings totalling 100 points:
    • Procedures (15 pts): Amendment in line with relevant procedure
    • Available Details (10 pts): MC100 and MC219 updated
    • Related Parties (10 pts): Third parties attended to
    • Workflow & Notes (15 pts): Adequate notes provided
    • Assessments Issued (5 pts): A/I manually completed where required
    • Suspense Managed (10 pts): Per Refund Procedure
    • Payee Details (10 pts): Refund details correct
    • Correspondence Channel (10 pts): Appropriate method used
    • Correspondence Content (10 pts): Accuracy of key details
    • Correspondence Style (5 pts): SRO Style Guide compliance
  4. Mark each component: YES (full points) or NO (0 points for that component)
  5. Calculate total score: Maximum 100 points
  6. Determine result: ≥95% = PASS | ≤94% = FAIL
  7. Record results in Quality Check Form
  8. Record results in e-Sys 'QA' tab of action item (ALWAYS record as PASS in e-Sys)
  9. Convey results to staff member via Response email or 1:1 feedback
PASS Threshold: 95% or greater = PASS (minimum KPI). This is the required accuracy standard.
FAIL Handling: If FAIL recorded, staff member makes corrections in e-Sys, quality checker confirms updates before passing.
Procedure Detail: Scoring & Weighting Logic▼

Weighting System

Percentage points are deducted when criteria not met. Total score = 100 minus deductions.

There is NO apportionment: either a component is fully completed (full points) or not (zero points). No partial credit.

Scoring Example

If an item has the following errors:

  • Procedures incomplete (−15 points)
  • Workflow notes inadequate (−15 points)
  • All other components correct

Total Score: 100 − 15 − 15 = 70% = FAIL

Result Determination

≥95%: PASS — Meets KPI standard

≤94%: FAIL — Does not meet standard, corrections required

Monthly Result Calculation

Individual monthly result = Average of all items checked (refund + random sample)

If average ≥95% = Monthly PASS; if ≤94% = Monthly FAIL

Input: List of items to check (refund >$10,000 + random sample), original action items in e-Sys
Process: Quality Checker evaluates each item against 9-component Quality Checklist, assigns points
Decision: Item scores ≥95% (PASS) or ≤94% (FAIL)?
Output: QA score for each item, results submitted to Form and e-Sys

Q4 - Record Results in Forms & e-Sys

Dual Recording Required

QA results MUST be recorded in TWO places: Quality Check Form and e-Sys.

Quality Check Form Recording

  1. Access Quality Check Form (Microsoft Form)
  2. For each assessed item, enter:
    • Staff member name
    • QA reason ("Random" for sample items, "Refund" for >$10,000)
    • Customer number
    • A/I type
    • Quality assessment (YES/NO for each of 9 checklist items)
    • QA comments (any issues noted)
  3. Submit form
  4. Results stored in secure, password-protected Excel spreadsheet
  5. If FAIL: Request "Receipt of response", provide PDF to staff member + Team Leader

e-Sys Recording (QA Tab)

  1. Open relevant action item in e-Sys
  2. Navigate to 'QA' tab
  3. Record:
    • QA date (date QA completed)
    • QA team member (who performed the QA)
    • Result: ALWAYS record as PASS (even if actual result was FAIL)
  4. If FAIL was recorded in Form: Staff member makes corrections in e-Sys, quality checker confirms updates
  5. Once corrections verified, QA marked as PASS in e-Sys
CRITICAL RULE: In e-Sys, QA tab must ALWAYS show PASS (regardless of actual assessment result). If item fails QA, staff member corrects in e-Sys first, then marked as PASS once verified.

Timeline for Recording

By 5th business day of following month: Team Leader confirms all results captured in QA Spreadsheet and team results calculated.

Procedure Detail: Adjusting Results▼

Result Adjustment Process

If a FAIL result is adjusted to PASS following discussion with staff member:

  • Quality checker advises relevant Team Leader
  • Team Leader adjusts source data in secure spreadsheet
  • Original FAIL result is superseded by adjusted PASS

Important Note

Adjustments should only occur after staff member makes corrections and quality checker verifies accuracy.

Input: QA assessment results (scores and PASS/FAIL decisions)
Process: Record in Quality Check Form and e-Sys QA tab simultaneously
Decision: Are results captured in both systems? Is e-Sys showing PASS? Are corrections verified?
Output: Results in secure spreadsheet, e-Sys updated, staff member notified

Q5 - Provide Feedback & Monthly Results

Feedback Timing & Responsibility

Due: By 5th business day of following month (via monthly 1:1 meeting)

Who: Team Leader (supported by Quality Checker for technical details)

Immediate Feedback (For FAIL Results)

  1. Quality Checker provides PDF receipt of FAIL result to staff member
  2. Email to staff member (CC Team Leader)
  3. Specifies: What was wrong, what needs correction, deadline for re-submission
  4. Staff member makes corrections in e-Sys
  5. Quality Checker confirms corrections are accurate

Monthly 1:1 Feedback Meeting

Topics covered:

  • Individual monthly QA result (%) and PASS/FAIL status
  • Team monthly QA results (aggregate) and PASS/FAIL
  • Items assessed during month (refund + random sample)
  • Common errors or trends identified
  • Positive performance acknowledgement
  • Areas for improvement going forward
  • Any coaching or mentoring needed

Monthly Report

Team Leader provides written monthly report showing:

  • Individual staff QA results (name, %, PASS/FAIL)
  • Team aggregate QA results
  • Trend analysis (improving/declining)
  • Action items for staff needing support
Key: Feedback should be provided immediately for FAIL results AND reinforced through monthly 1:1 meetings and written reports. Constructive approach focusing on improvement.
Procedure Detail: Feedback Best Practices▼

Immediate Feedback (FAIL Items)

Provide specific details: which checklist items failed, why, and what the staff member needs to do to correct.

Positive Feedback

Acknowledge items that scored 100% (no errors). Reinforce correct processes.

Trend Recognition

If same errors recurring (e.g., always missing notes, always incomplete procedures), address pattern in 1:1 meeting and provide targeted coaching.

Monthly 1:1 Meeting Timing

Conduct by 5th business day of following month to allow time for staff member to review results and prepare questions.

Input: QA results spreadsheet, individual and team monthly scores
Process: Team Leader calculates metrics, provides immediate feedback for FAILs, conducts 1:1 meeting
Decision: Is feedback constructive? Does staff understand what to improve? Are results documented?
Output: Staff member receives feedback, results recorded, trends identified

Quality Checklist & Weightings

Component Checklist Item Expectation Weighting
Procedures Procedure Compliance Amendment completed in line with relevant procedure (PPR, PPL, L/O, Refund, etc.). MLT Assisted Digital used for basic assessments. 15
Available Details Updated MC100 (Name, Title, DOB, email, phone) and MC219 (Address) updated 10
Related Parties Attended Matters for related/third parties addressed per procedure 10
Documentation Workflow & Notes Adequate notes in Comments field (Reference tab): what done, result, money allocated, assessment effect, correspondence method, user ID, date 15
Assessment Assessments Issued Assessments issued where A/I manually completed 5
Suspense Suspense Managed Suspense allocated to liabilities, transferred (with authority), refunded (with interest if applicable), or transferred to treasury trust 10
Payee Details Confirmed Payee details correct, delivery address/EFT details correct, A/I reference populated in refund W/I 10
Correspondence Appropriate Channel Email for MLT registrants and electronic lodgements 10
Content Accuracy Correct person, dates, properties, years, refund amounts, interest, legislation 10
Style & Grammar SRO Style Guide compliant, correct spelling/grammar, Land Revenue Administration Branch referenced 5
TOTAL POINTS 100

Scoring Rules

  • YES for component = Full points | NO for component = 0 points (no partial credit)
  • Total score = 100 minus deductions for NO responses
  • ≥95% = PASS | ≤94% = FAIL
  • Minimum acceptable accuracy KPI: 95%

Sample Calculation Examples

Example 1: Staff Member 'A' - PASS Result

Refund Items (>$10,000): 10 completed, all 10 correct = 100%

Random Sample: 5 items selected, all 5 correct = 100%

Monthly Result: (100% + 100%) / 2 = 100% = PASS

Example 2: Staff Member 'B' - FAIL Result

Refund Items (>$10,000): 6 completed, 5 correct = 83.3%

Random Sample: 5 items selected, all 5 correct = 100%

Monthly Result: (83.3% + 100%) / 2 = 91.65% = FAIL

Action: Provide coaching, identify what went wrong in the 1 failed refund item, reinforce procedures.

Example 3: Single Item Scoring

Item has errors in: Procedures (−15), Workflow Notes (−15), all others correct

Score: 100 − 15 − 15 = 70% = FAIL

Feedback: "Procedures not followed correctly and notes inadequate. Please review the PPR/PPL procedure and re-do with full documentation."
