SRO Transformation Architecture
Strategic Initiative: Single Customer Portal
The portal is not a service channel built on top of clean data. The portal is the mechanism that creates clean data — by establishing a verified digital relationship with customers before they become liable.
v3.0 — April 2026
Core strategic shift: The previous framing positioned the portal as a downstream service channel that depended on back-end data cleansing as a prerequisite. This version inverts that logic. The portal is the primary intervention — a mandatory registration point that captures verified customer identity before the Notice of Acquisition arrives. The customer becomes the data quality mechanism. Back-end legacy cleanup runs in parallel, not as a gate.
01
Problem Statement

The SRO does not have a digital relationship with land tax customers. First contact typically occurs after a Notice of Acquisition is received from Land Use Victoria — at which point the system must identify, match, and verify the customer under time pressure, using incomplete or conflicting data.

This reactive model generates the single largest category of operational exceptions in Land Tax. The Pareto analysis of 93,000+ work items shows:

Root cause: The SRO discovers who the customer is after the obligation exists. If the customer were already known, verified, and digitally contactable before the NOA, the majority of these exceptions would not occur.
02
Strategic Intent

Establish a mandatory digital registration portal that captures verified customer identity, contact details, communication preferences, and property/trust relationships before the Notice of Acquisition triggers the Land Tax lifecycle.

When the NOA arrives from Land Use Victoria, the system matches it against an already-verified customer record — eliminating party match exceptions, reducing FTI investigations, and ensuring assessments issue to the right person at the right address on the first attempt.

```
Step 1
Customer Registers on Portal
Self-service identity verification. Matches or creates SRO record.
Step 2
Customer Confirms Details
Address, contact, communication preference, trust/joint ownership declared.
Step 3
NOA Received from LUV
System matches NOA to verified portal record. Clean match = no exception.
Step 4
Assessment Issues Cleanly
Right person, right address, digital delivery. No withdrawal, no return.
```
03
Design Principles
01
Mandatory Registration at Ownership
Portal registration is required during the land ownership notification phase. This is the policy lever that ensures every future liable customer has a verified digital record before assessment.
02
Customer as Data Quality Mechanism
The customer verifies their own identity, confirms their details, and declares their ownership/trust structures. This replaces back-office investigation with self-service capture at source.
03
Match Before Liability
The portal record must be matchable to incoming NOA data. If the match is clean, no exception is generated. The goal is zero-touch for the majority of new land acquisitions.
04
Digital-First Communication
Portal registration captures electronic consent. All subsequent communication defaults to digital. Paper is the exception, not the default. This eliminates returned mail at source.
05
Progressive Value — Not Big Bang
Each phase delivers measurable backlog reduction independently. The portal doesn't wait for perfection — it starts reducing exceptions from the first registration.
06
Legacy Cleanup in Parallel
Existing data quality issues are addressed through a parallel remediation track. The portal stops the bleeding; the cleanup heals the wound. Neither waits for the other.
04

05
Why This Model Works
  • Stops the bleeding immediately. Every new registration creates a clean record. You don't need to fix all existing data before the portal delivers value — you just need to stop creating new exceptions.
  • Uses the customer as the data source. Instead of back-office staff investigating who a customer is, the customer self-identifies. This is faster, cheaper, and more accurate than any data cleansing program.
  • FTI investigations become preventable. The 9,594-item FTI queue exists because customer identity is unknown after liability. Mandated registration before ownership means identity is already confirmed when the NOA arrives.
  • LOAN exceptions reduce structurally. Party match and property conflict exceptions (15,147 items) occur because the system tries to match incoming NOA data against incomplete records. A pre-verified portal record gives the system a clean match target.
  • No dependency gate. The portal is not waiting for a back-end project to finish. It delivers value from the first registration. Legacy cleanup runs alongside — not before.
  • Scales beyond Land Tax. The portal and registration model is a platform capability. Once proven for Land Tax, it extends to Payroll Tax, Land Transfer Duty, and other revenue lines.

09
Executive Decision

This initiative inverts the traditional approach. Instead of cleansing data first and building the portal later, it uses the portal as the data capture mechanism — mandating customer registration at the point of land ownership so verified records exist before liability.

What changes:

• FTI investigations (9,594 items) become preventable — customer identity is known before liability triggers
• LOAN party match exceptions (15,147 items) become structurally reduced — the system has a clean record to match against
• Withdrawn assessments (7,467 items) become avoidable — verified address and contact details before issue
• Returned mail (4,636 items) becomes eliminated — digital communication by default from registration

Total addressable backlog: ~60,500 items — 65% of all Land Tax work items.

Legacy data cleanup runs in parallel and heals the existing back book. But the portal is the intervention that stops creating new exceptions — and it starts delivering value from the first registration, not after a multi-year data remediation programme completes.

The portal is not the destination. It is the mechanism.
