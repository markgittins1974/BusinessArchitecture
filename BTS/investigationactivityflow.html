Activity # Activity Name Description Pain Point User Role
VALUE STAGE 1: INTAKE & VALIDATION
1.1 Receive PIC / Trigger Receive a PIC, referral (IR), anomaly, or system‑generated trigger from internal or external sources. Triggers originate from multiple channels and systems, often requiring investigators to conduct additional background searches to identify missing or supporting information before assessment. PICs arrive through multiple channels with inconsistent structure and quality. Key information is often missing and must be found via separate searches (e.g., ATO rental history, RTBA history). IRs may arrive with comments but no supporting evidence attached. Investigator, Team Leader, Third-Party Data Provider
1.1-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception Receive land tax‑related triggers including council valuation anomalies, land use mismatches, vacant residential land indicators, and trust ownership irregularities, often sourced from third‑party or council data. Council data used to trigger investigations is often late, inconsistent, or outdated. Municipal ratings frequently do not reflect current property status (e.g. properties listed as active when demolished), leading to false positives and unnecessary investigative effort Investigator, Team Leader
1.1-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception Receive payroll tax triggers such as STP discrepancies, VWA wage mismatches, grouping anomalies, abnormal payroll growth, and contractor engagement indicators. STP data is inconsistent and rarely aligns with employer‑reported information, reducing trust in triggers and requiring extensive manual reconciliation before validation can occur. Investigator, Team Leader
1.1-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception Receive FHOG triggers including suspicious grant applications, builder anomalies, high‑risk postcode indicators, identity mismatches, and occupancy‑related alerts. FHOG fraud patterns are highly variable and often novel, making triggers difficult to standardise and increasing the reliance on investigator judgement during early validation Investigator, Team Leader
1.1-Duties ◇ Duties Exception Receive duties‑related triggers such as conveyancer referral anomalies, undervaluation indicators, foreign purchaser flags, related‑party transfers, and complex trust structures. Conveyancer referrals and uploads are often incomplete or lack full disclosure, requiring investigators to triangulate information across systems and documents before validation can occur Investigator, Team Leader
1.2 Validate Trigger Assess whether the received PIC or IR meets investigation thresholds, or whether it should be redirected or closed based on evidence sufficiency and relevance. A significant proportion of PICs do not warrant investigation, but inconsistent intake criteria and differing interpretations of evidence thresholds across branches result in misrouted work. Newer revenue‑line PICs (e.g. VRLT) are frequently closed without action due to inconsistent vetting standards Investigator, Team Leader, Legal / Policy Advisor
1.3 Create/Update Case Record Create or update the investigation case record, attach documentation, and capture metadata after completing initial background searches. Investigators enter the same information multiple times across systems (eSys, CM10/CM9, email). Documents are commonly saved to desktops, uploaded to CM10, and then manually referenced in eSys, increasing administrative effort and the risk of inconsistency and error. Case creation typically occurs only after background searches, delaying system traceability Investigator, Team Leader, Processor
Operational reality note: Although the designed process shows case creation (1.3) before triage and background checks, in practice investigators often complete Initial Checks & Background Searches and informal risk assessment (2.1–2.3) before creating a formal investigation case. This avoids opening low‑value investigations but results in substantive work being undertaken outside the system of record.
VALUE STAGE 2: TRIAGE & PRIORITISATION
2.1 Initial Checks & Background Searches (NOTE Done before IC Creation) Perform preliminary checks to understand the customer, entity structure, history, and potential risk indicators. These checks are commonly completed before a formal investigation case is opened, with information stored locally for later use. Investigators spend significant time conducting manual searches across multiple systems and external sources. Data must be repeatedly copied between systems due to lack of integration. System constraints (e.g. search limits, time conversion issues, context switching) add friction, and key identifiers (PIC, CID, LID) must be manually pasted between tools. Much of this work is done outside formal case records, reducing traceability. Investigator, Team Leader, Peer Reviewer
2.1-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception For Land Tax exceptions, verify land use classification, ownership structure, and cross-check against Duties/Titles; prioritise immediate “big 3” checks (DLS, ATO, RTBA). Valuation/council datasets can conflict. In practice, valuation history is only relied on when values appear anomalous (e.g., $0), but mismatches still create investigation noise and rework. Investigator, Team Leader
2.1-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception Cross-check VWA vs PTX wages, analyse STP payroll totals, identify grouping relationships, review contractor reclassification risk Grouping is a nightmare — there's no single source of truth. Investigator, Team Leader
2.1-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception Verify occupancy using utilities data, cross-check identity documents, validate builder registration, check for multiple grants We rely on utilities data but it's slow and inconsistent. Investigator, Team Leader
2.1-Duties ◇ Duties Exception Verify property transfer against Titles Office, check valuation for undervaluation, identify foreign purchaser surcharge, review related-party docs Duties Online data is good, but conveyancer uploads are inconsistent. Investigator, Team Leader
2.2 Assess Risk & Determine Priority Determine risk, complexity, and urgency to prioritise the case—leveraging PIC/IR vetting inputs, then confirming whether the matter should proceed, pause, or redirect. Risk and complexity assessments vary by person. Complexity is often pre-defined during PIC/IR vetting, yet downstream handling still differs and investigators frequently request specific PIC/IR types, contributing to inconsistent prioritisation. Investigator, Team Leader, Executive Reviewer
2.3 Proceed / Redirect / Close (DECISION GATE) Decision gate: based on risk and available evidence, decide to proceed to planning, redirect, or close the case early. There is a strong default assumption that PICs and IRs should proceed unless evidence clearly disproves the trigger. Case value is not a factor in decision‑making, leading to low‑value cases progressing unnecessarily and remaining open longer than needed. Investigator, Team Leader, Executive Reviewer
VALUE STAGE 3: INVESTIGATION PLANNING
3.1 Define Scope & Objectives Define what the investigation will examine, what evidence is required, and the potential outcomes; at around this point investigators often update their personal file-tracking spreadsheet. Investigations often commence without a structured plan. Scope definition varies widely, and investigators maintain personal tracking spreadsheets on desktops that are not standardised, shared, or system‑based, increasing risk during handover or review. Investigator, Team Leader, Legal / Policy Advisor
3.1-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception Determine land use, assess exemption eligibility, identify trust structures/beneficiaries, determine entity grouping land exemption issues then require VLRT consideration. Investigator, Team Leader
3.1-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception Determine grouping across entities, assess contractor vs employee classification, verify interstate allocation, identify exempt wages Contractor rules are interpreted differently by everyone. Investigator, Team Leader
3.1-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception Confirm genuine occupancy timeframe, validate first-home buyer eligibility, assess builder legitimacy, identify straw-buyer arrangements Occupancy is the hardest thing to prove. Investigator, Team Leader
3.1-Duties ◇ Duties Exception Determine correct dutiable value, assess related-party transfer legitimacy, determine foreign surcharge applicability, assess landholder duty Related-party transfers are always messy. Investigator, Team Leader
3.2 Develop Investigation Plan Create a structured plan outlining tasks, evidence sources, timelines, and dependencies Investigation plans are rarely documented. Planning is predominantly implicit and investigator‑driven, reducing transparency, auditability, and consistency across investigations.. Lack of documented plans makes handovers difficult. Investigator, Team Leader, Legal / Policy Advisor
3.3 Obtain Approvals Seek approvals for scope, planned actions, or use of powers when required—most commonly only when deviating significantly from what the PIC already indicates. Approvals are largely email-based and can be slow when needed, with limited auditability. In many cases approvals are avoided because scope is treated as predetermined by the PIC. While most investigations proceed based on the original PIC scope, any deviation introduces delays and limited audit traceability due to manual approval processes. Team Leader, Executive Reviewer, Legal / Policy Advisor
VALUE STAGE 4: EVIDENCE & ANALYSIS
4.1 Gather Evidence Collect evidence from internal systems, external agencies, customers, and third parties—although much evidence gathering may occur earlier during background checks. Evidence is often gathered multiple times and stored inconsistently. A major challenge is determining the best available address for correspondence. Customer responses vary significantly and often include unusable formats (large protected PDFs, ZIP files, inaccessible links, or documents without explanatory text), increasing review effort and delays. Investigator, Team Leader, Third-Party Data Provider
4.1-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception For Land Tax exceptions, collect council rates/valuation documents, land use declarations, site evidence, trust deeds, and Titles extracts—often requiring council engagement. Customers are asked for documents councils already hold. Councils may be unhelpful and requests can require approval; this pain point is common across revenue lines. Investigator, Team Leader
4.1-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception For Payroll Tax exceptions, collect payroll reports, STP submissions, VWA declarations, contractor agreements, and allocation breakdowns in usable formats. Employers provide payroll data in many inconsistent formats. Similar evidence-handling issues apply across revenue lines, increasing time spent standardising and validating documents. Investigator, Team Leader
4.1-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception For FHOG exceptions, collect occupancy/utility evidence, identity documents, lease/bank statements, builder contracts, and supporting records to validate eligibility. Applicants may provide altered/doctored documents, requiring verification against independent sources. Document quality and accessibility issues are common across revenue lines. Investigator, Team Leader
4.1-Duties ◇ Duties Exception For Duties exceptions, collect contracts of sale, adjustments, valuations, Titles/trust/corporate documents, declarations, and financial evidence required for assessment. Large contracts/packages are often hard to navigate (no index, protected PDFs). Evidence can arrive in inconsistent, hard-to-use formats—an issue seen across revenue lines. Investigator, Team Leader
4.2 Analyse Evidence & Identify Gaps Review collected evidence, identify inconsistencies, and determine what additional information is required We don't know what we already have — everything is buried in CM9. Poor document organisation makes evidence hard to locate. Inconsistant naming of documents already located in Cm10 makes it painful to look through. Recording of investigation actions in eSys is tedious and inconsistant in time recording and categorisation. Investigator, Team Leader, Peer Reviewer
4.3 Request Additional Information Send formal or informal requests for additional information to customers or external agencies, using established letter templates where available. While templates exist for many scenarios, there is little automation. Requests and follow-ups are still largely manual, increasing cycle time and inconsistency. Investigator, Team Leader, Third-Party Data Provider
4.4 Conduct Interviews or Site Visits Conduct interviews, site visits, or inspections to gather further evidence or validate claims There is no standardised mechanism or template to record interviews or site visits, leading to inconsistent evidence capture and reliance on free‑text notes. Investigator, Team Leader, Legal / Policy Advisor
4.5 Form Preliminary View Based on evidence gathered, form a preliminary view of liability, compliance, or risk Everyone interprets evidence differently. Lack of standardised assessment criteria leads to inconsistent outcomes. Investigator, Team Leader, Peer Reviewer
VALUE STAGE 5: ENFORCEMENT & RESOLUTION
5.1 Draft Findings & Notices Prepare draft findings, notices, or assessments based on evidence—often iterating between drafting and additional evidence gathering as gaps emerge. Drafting overlaps with evidence gathering and is highly individualised. Templates vary in structure and quality, increasing inconsistency and legal risk. Investigator, Team Leader, Processor, Legal / Policy Advisor
5.1-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception Land Tax Assessment Notice, Vacant Residential Land Tax Notice, Land Use Adjustment Notice, Trust Surcharge Assessment LT notices are the most complex — too many variations. Investigator, Team Leader
5.1-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception Payroll Tax Assessment, Grouping Determination Notice, Contractor Reclassification Notice, Interstate Payroll Adjustment Notice Grouping notices are the most contested — employers always push back. Investigator, Team Leader
5.1-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception FHOG Reversal Notice, FHOG Recovery Notice, FHOG Eligibility Determination, Occupancy Breach Notice FHOG reversals get messy — people get emotional. Investigator, Team Leader
5.1-Duties ◇ Duties Exception Duties Assessment Notice, Foreign Purchaser Surcharge Assessment, Landholder Duty Assessment, Off-the-Plan Concession Adjustment Notice Foreign purchaser surcharge is the most contested part. Investigator, Team Leader
5.2 Review & Approve Findings Review draft findings and notices for accuracy, consistency, and legal compliance; once approved, update the relevant case systems accordingly. Reviews are manual and time‑consuming. Once approved, updates to eSys are completed manually, further increasing administrative effort and risk of error Team Leader, Executive Reviewer, Legal / Policy Advisor
5.3 Issue Notices / Assessments Issue formal notices/assessments to the customer and file them against the case, ensuring system records reflect the issuance. Issuance and filing are manual. Assessments may be generated manually and penalty calculations are often performed in separate spreadsheets, increasing error risk and time burden. Investigator, Team Leader, Processor
5.4 Objections Received? (DECISION GATE) Decision gate: determine whether objections have been received and manage the response—closing the IC when the outcome letter/assessment is issued, and handling objections through the appropriate pathway. Objections can significantly extend timelines due to back‑and‑forth communication and the absence of a structured objections workflow. Objections may be received directly via email to investigators or submitted through formal forms routed via a review team. In both cases, objections are often manually created or recorded in eSys, introducing delays, duplication, and inconsistent handling. Investigators may be asked to amend decisions or manually log objections after case closure, reducing traceability and standardisation. Investigator, Team Leader, Legal / Policy Advisor
5.4-LT 🔸 Land Tax Exception For Land Tax objections, assess disputes such as land use, valuation, trust surcharge, exemption changes, grouping, and primary production usage. Valuation objections can be slow and dependent on other teams, but in practice they may be deprioritised and processed regardless, risking misalignment between dependency timelines and case handling. Investigator, Team Leader
5.4-PTX ◆ Payroll Tax Exception Disputes: grouping determination, contractor classification, wage totals (STP vs employer), interstate allocation, exempt wages Objections turn into mini-audits — we basically redo the whole case. Investigator, Team Leader
5.4-FHOG ★ FHOG Exception Disputes: occupancy evidence, claims of temporary absence, builder contract validity, identity verification, administrative error claims Objections often turn into emotional appeals rather than factual disputes. Investigator, Team Leader
5.4-Duties ◇ Duties Exception Disputes: dutiable value, foreign purchaser surcharge, related-party valuation, landholder thresholds, off-the-plan concession eligibility Duties objections are technical — they take ages to resolve. Investigator, Team Leader
VALUE STAGE 6: LEARNING & IMPROVEMENT
6.1 Capture Learnings & Insights Document key learnings, systemic issues, and opportunities for improvement from the case, and capture them in a reusable format. Learnings are rarely captured in shared systems. Many investigators keep personal templates and reference notes in OneNote, which limits organisational visibility and continuous improvement. Investigator, Team Leader, Executive Reviewer
6.2 Share Insights with Other Teams Share insights with other teams to improve processes, risk models, and compliance strategies Insights never go anywhere — no one sees them. No structured mechanism for sharing insights limits organisational learning. Investigator, Team Leader, Executive Reviewer, Legal / Policy Advisor
