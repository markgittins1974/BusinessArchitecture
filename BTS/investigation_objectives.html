🧩 Success Measure 1: Faster Resolution Without Increased Risk
🟢

Ensure cases are complete at intake

How might we ensure cases are complete at intake so that investigators begin work with all required information, reducing delays, rework, and unnecessary back-and-forth with customers.

🔗 Dependencies:

📋 Intake process agreement (ops decision) - Must lock by EOW Week 1
✓ Completeness checklist design (1 week)
✓ Salesforce validation rules (1-2 weeks)
Example

A checklist at intake validates: case facts entered, PIK details linked, initial source documents attached. Missing fields trigger a return to the caller before case creation in Salesforce, eliminating rework five steps later.

🟢

Eliminate duplicate data entry

How might we eliminate duplicate data entry so that investigators spend less time re-keying information and more time applying judgement, improving both speed and accuracy.

🔗 Dependencies:

⚠️ ESYS integration API feasibility - MUST confirm by EOW Week 1. Risk of 4-8 week slip or descope.
📋 ESYS data mapping (1-2 weeks) - Confirm ESYS contact assigned
✓ Salesforce auto-population rules (1-2 weeks)
✓ Integration testing & reconciliation (2-3 weeks)
Example

PIK data from ESYS auto-populates Salesforce case fields (complainant, subject, initial facts). Investigators verify rather than re-key, reducing intake time from 45 mins to 10 mins per case.

🟢

Single consistent intake pathway

How might we ensure every case enters through a single, consistent pathway so that work is routed correctly from the start, reducing misclassification and ensuring clear ownership.

🔗 Dependencies:

📋 Intake process agreement - Must lock by EOW Week 1
✓ Intake form design (1 week)
✓ Salesforce workflow automation (2-3 weeks)
✓ Intake team training (1 week)
Example

All PICs (from complaints, referrals, COI) flow through a single intake form in Salesforce. Case type, risk tier, and investigator assignment are determined by rules at intake, not ad hoc reassignments later.

🟢

Identify low-value cases early

How might we identify low-value or non-actionable cases early so that investigators can focus on high-value work and avoid progressing cases that will not lead to meaningful outcomes.

🔗 Dependencies:

📋 "Low-value" criteria definition (ops decision) - Must lock by EOW Week 2
✓ Triage workflow design (1 week)
✓ Salesforce triage form & closure rules (2 weeks)
Example

At triage, a case flagged as "no identifiable subject" or "statute-barred" is closed with a closure code, not handed to an investigator. 30% of cases historically closed this way; effort reallocation improves high-value case velocity.

🟠

Bring all relevant information to triage

How might we bring all relevant information together at triage so that triage decisions are made quickly and confidently, without investigators needing to search across multiple systems.

Why Orange: Requires data integration from multiple legacy systems (ESYS, CM9, BIB, COI). API complexity moderate; needs clear data mapping and validation rules before build.

Example

Triage view aggregates: prior investigations, payroll data, COI flags, complaint history. Triage officer makes decision in one system view, not by querying CM9, ESYS, and BIB separately.

🔴

Make triage decisions consistent across sources

How might we make triage decisions consistent across systems and data sources so that the same case receives the same decision regardless of where its data originates, improving fairness and defensibility.

Why Red: Requires deterministic risk scoring algorithm and real-time data consistency across ESYS/Salesforce/BIB. Needs extensive validation and testing to ensure fairness and auditability. High complexity in rule definition and edge-case handling.

Example

Two cases with identical risk profiles (high payroll discrepancy, prior non-compliance) should receive same priority tier whether data comes from a tip-off or a referral. Risk scoring logic enforces this; audit trail shows why.

🟢

Make investigation plans explicit & traceable

How might we make investigation plans explicit and traceable so that investigators have a clear roadmap of required steps, reducing ambiguity, rework, and delays.

🔗 Dependencies:

📋 Plan template design (ops) - Low complexity, 1-2 weeks
✓ Salesforce form & workflow (1-2 weeks)
✓ Investigator training (1 week)
Example

Case record includes a plan stage: "1. Obtain payroll reconciliation from subject, 2. Interview subject, 3. Obtain bank records, 4. Draft memo." Completed steps auto-populate case timeline; approval gates at memo stage.

🔴

Make evidence easy to find

How might we make evidence easy to find regardless of where it is stored so that investigators can quickly locate the information they need, even when evidence is distributed across multiple repositories.

Why Red: Cross-system search spanning CM9, TRIM, email, evidence portal, and external repositories. Requires semantic search/indexing, OCR for scanned documents, and API integration with legacy systems. High volume of unstructured data and varying formats compound complexity.

Example

Search from case record returns results from: attached documents, TRIM folder, email (via API), evidence portal uploads. Investigators type once; no manual searches across five systems.

🧩 Success Measure 2: Consistent and Defensible Outcomes
🟠

Apply a consistent risk framework

How might we apply a consistent risk framework so that prioritisation decisions are fair, repeatable, and aligned to organisational risk appetite.

Why Orange: Requires Enriched PIC List (gates this work) + framework definition (ops decision). Framework can use PIC-only data (v1.0) but full value requires enriched data (prior PICs, non-compliance, sector risk). With Enriched PIC List available Week 8: framework v1.0 Week 9, full v2.0 Week 12+ (Phase 2).

🔗 Dependencies:

🚨 Enriched PIC List (Data Lake) - Week 8, gates all framework work. Must contain COI + ESYS + ASIC + SRO data.
📋 Risk framework DEFINITION (ops decision) - Must lock by EOW Week 2. If not pre-defined, add 4-6 weeks.
⚠️ Data validation (technical) - Confirm enriched data quality, completeness, freshness before framework formulas depend on it (Week 7-8).
✓ Salesforce formula/rules engine (1-2 weeks) - Only after Enriched PIC List flows to SF
✓ Validation testing & fairness review (1-2 weeks)
Example

v1.0 Framework (Week 9): Scores based on complaint type (+3 for wage theft), amount ($50k +2), source (employee +1), specificity (+1), flags. Result: Tier 2. v2.0 Framework (Phase 2, Week 12+): Same complaint gets boost for repeat offender (+3), sector risk (+1), prior non-compliance (+2). Result: Tier 1. Both powered by Enriched PIC List.

🟠

Maintain a single authoritative case record

How might we maintain a single authoritative case record so that investigators and reviewers always work from the same source of truth, reducing errors and inconsistencies.

Why Orange: Requires two-system ownership model (ESYS for PIC, Salesforce for full lifecycle) with bidirectional sync via MuleSoft APIs. Needs reconciliation logic for conflicts and state machine enforcement. Moderate complexity; manageable with clear data governance.

Example

Salesforce case is the single record of truth. Work in CM9 syncs back via API; no divergence between "source of record" (ESYS) and "case details" (Salesforce). Investigators, managers, and auditors see same view.

🔴

Improve consistency in evidence interpretation

How might we improve consistency in evidence interpretation so that similar cases lead to similar outcomes, reducing variation and strengthening defensibility.

Why Red: Requires codification of investigative judgment rules and post-hoc outcome analysis to detect patterns and outliers. Needs AI-assisted evidence synthesis to enable consistent comparison. Involves organizational change (skill alignment across teams) and extensive validation before enforcement.

Example

Two investigators assessing payroll discrepancy reach different conclusions. Post-hoc analysis shows one applied a 5% tolerance, the other 10%. Framework codifies: "Discrepancy >7.5% requires investigation." Future cases follow this rule; decisions become comparable.

🔴

Provide a complete, defensible decision trail

How might we provide a complete, defensible decision trail so that every decision can be traced back to the evidence and reasoning that informed it, reducing audit burden and overturn risk.

Why Red: Gating dependency across multiple outcomes. Requires complete audit logging across ESYS, Salesforce, and all integrations. Must capture user actions, system state changes, evidence timestamps, and reasoning at decision points. Complex to implement without performance impact. Critical for defensibility but architecturally foundational.

Example

Assessment memo auto-links: facts entered, evidence attached, risk score calculated, decision made. Objection reviewer sees the chain; if facts were wrong, admits evidence reanalysis. If reasoning was sound, defends decision quickly.

🟢

Resolve objections without re-investigating

How might we resolve objections without re-doing investigations so that investigators can respond efficiently to objections using existing analysis rather than repeating work.

🔗 Dependencies:

→ Depends on HMW #1-#3 (intake completeness, consistent pathway). Build in parallel, deploy after.
✓ Objection workflow design (1 week)
✓ Salesforce objection form & linking (2 weeks)
Example

Objection received. Reviewer opens original case record, evidence, and memo. Objection raises new fact; reviewer determines if fact was available at investigation (missed) or is new. If missed, targeted reanalysis. If new, treats as post-decision change.

🔴

Help interpret complex evidence bundles

How might we help investigators interpret large, complex evidence bundles so that complex cases can be analysed more quickly and consistently, reducing cognitive load and improving quality.

Why Red: Requires advanced AI (document extraction, anomaly detection, multi-format data synthesis). Must handle structured data (payroll spreadsheets) and unstructured text (emails, scanned docs). High accuracy requirements for defensibility; extensive testing needed before operational use.

Example

Investigator uploads 50 payroll spreadsheets from five years. AI extracts salary history, flags anomalies, structures data into a comparison table. Investigator focuses on judgment: does pattern indicate non-compliance? Not data extraction.

🟢

Streamline approvals

How might we streamline approvals so that oversight is maintained without slowing down progress or relying on email-based processes.

Example

Assessment memo draft flagged for review in Salesforce. Approver notified, reviews, approves or rejects with comments. Timeline recorded. No email chains; audit trail is automatic. Approval turnaround drops from 5 days to 1 day.

🧩 Success Measure 4: Learning System That Improves Over Time
🟢

Capture and reuse learnings

How might we capture and reuse learnings so that insights from past cases improve future decisions and reduce repeated mistakes.

🔗 Dependencies:

📋 Learning taxonomy design (ops/KM) - 1-2 weeks. Keep simple (10-15 tags) to avoid scope creep.
✓ Salesforce tagging UI (1-2 weeks)
✓ Search/filter on tags (1-2 weeks)
⚠️ Adoption (ongoing) - Investigators must use tagging consistently for value
Example

Closed case marked "education provider, false payroll claim pattern." Tag logged. Next case on education provider auto-surfaces similar pattern in knowledge base. Investigator learns: "This sector has a known scam." Repeating mistakes avoided.

🟢

Create structured insight-sharing mechanisms

How might we create structured mechanisms to share insights so that teams benefit from each other's experience rather than relying on informal knowledge transfer.

🔗 Dependencies:

📋 Insight definition & approval process (ops/KM) - 1-2 weeks. Define narrowly to avoid scope creep.
✓ Insight capture form design (1 week)
✓ Salesforce form & approval workflow (2-3 weeks)
⚠️ Insights repository (simple list, not dashboards in Phase 1 - 2-3 weeks)
Example

Monthly insights report: "Ten cases this month involved payroll inflation in hospitality sector. Suggest updating risk framework to flag this sector + payroll variance." Teams see systematic patterns, not anecdotes from the water cooler.

🟠

Ensure insights flow back into risk models

How might we ensure insights flow back into risk models so that risk scoring and prioritisation improve over time based on real investigative outcomes.

Why Orange: Requires feedback loop: outcome analysis → pattern detection → model reweighting → validation. Needs data governance and model governance (testing, approval gates). Moderate complexity; procedural/organizational challenge more than technical.

Example

Risk model flags 20% of cases as high-priority. Audit at month-end: 60% of those led to enforcement action. Meanwhile, 10% of low-priority cases also led to action. Model reweighting: increase payroll variance factor, lower sector-based assumptions.

🔴

Index and classify evidence automatically

How might we index and classify evidence automatically so that investigators spend less time organising documents and more time analysing them.

Why Red: Requires document classification ML model, OCR for scanned files, and integration with evidence repositories. Must handle diverse document types (payroll, emails, contracts, bank statements). Accuracy critical for investigator trust; needs continuous retraining and human feedback loops.

Example

Investigator uploads evidence bundle. AI classifies: "3 payroll documents, 5 emails, 2 bank statements, 1 contract." Organizes into timeline, highlights discrepancies. Investigator opens already-organized view; no manual sorting.

🧩 Success Measure 5: AI-Enabled Success
🟢

Support investigators with AI-assisted insight

How might we support investigators with AI-assisted insight so that investigators can synthesise information faster and make more informed decisions.

🔗 Dependencies:

✓ Claude API access (assume ready) - 1 day to enable
✓ Salesforce API integration (1-2 weeks)
✓ Prompt design & testing (1-2 weeks)
🔒 Governance/ethics review (1-2 weeks) - May be slow; use sandbox pilot to mitigate
Example

Investigator asks AI: "Summarize the payroll pattern across all attached documents." AI synthesizes five years of data and flags: "Salary increased 50% in month three, no corresponding role change documented." Investigator focuses on: is this legitimate?

🟠

Ensure AI is used safely and appropriately

How might we ensure AI is used safely and appropriately so that AI enhances decision-making without introducing new risks or compliance issues.

Why Orange: Requires policy definition, system controls (guardrails), and audit mechanisms. Needs cross-functional input (legal, compliance, operational). Moderate technical lift; primarily organizational/governance challenge. Must be solved before AI features go operational.

Example

AI recommendations flagged as "informational only; human decision required." Investigator cannot close case based on AI output alone. All AI-informed decisions auditable. Policy enforcement in system: override requires approval.

🟠

Detect and manage AI drift or bias

How might we detect and manage AI drift or bias so that AI-supported decisions remain fair, reliable, and aligned to policy over time.

Why Orange: Requires ongoing monitoring dashboards, outcome analysis pipelines, and retraining workflows. Needs fairness metrics definition and audit procedures. Procedurally complex but technically manageable. Must be implemented in parallel with AI features; continuous operational cost.

Example

Monthly audit: compare AI recommendations to actual investigation outcomes. If AI is recommending "high risk" for certain property types at 2x the rate of closure, investigate why. Retrain or adjust weights. Bias metrics tracked.

🔴

Combine extraction and reasoning models

How might we combine extraction and reasoning models so that AI can handle both structured extraction and higher-order judgement in complex cases.

Why Red: Highest technical complexity. Requires chaining extraction models (structured data) with reasoning models (narrative synthesis). Needs extensive testing for accuracy, hallucination detection, and edge-case handling. Model selection, fine-tuning, and guardrails are critical. Emerging technology with ongoing research; requires experimentation and validation.

Example

Complex case: 200-page document set. Extraction model pulls structured facts (dates, amounts, names). Reasoning model connects facts into narrative (e.g., "X moved salary to spouse's company, Y"). Investigator reviews synthesis, not raw data.
