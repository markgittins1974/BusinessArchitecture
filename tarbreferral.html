TARB Referrals & Determinations for LRA

Tax Advisory & Review Branch (TARB) Referrals and Determinations for Land Revenue Administration (LRA) • Revised: 26 November 2025

Q1 Consultation & Approval Q2 Matter Classification Q3 Hardship Relief Q4 Referral to TARB Q5 TARB Determination Q6 Post-Amendment & Close
```

Q1 – Has consultation and approval been obtained prior to referring the matter to TARB?

  • Consult with a Branch Technical Officer (BTO) within LRA.
  • If BTO is unavailable, consult with an experienced Senior Customer Service Officer, a TARB Liaison Officer, and/or the Team Leader.
  • Obtain approval to proceed with referral.
  • Confirm whether action item type needs changing to Formal Objection or Private Ruling — if so, the TARB Referral Officer must action the change.
  • This process covers both LTX and VRLT revenue lines.
Do not proceed with referral without approval from a TARB Referral Officer.
Procedure reference: Introduction, background & how to refer to TARB
Input data:
  • Customer correspondence
  • Referral context (nature of matter, relevant tax years, customer details)
  • Revenue line (LTX or VRLT)
Processing responsibility:
  • LRA staff — initiate consultation
  • BTO / Senior CSO / TARB Liaison Officer / Team Leader — approve or reject referral
  • TARB Referral Officer — change action item type if required
Decision: Referral gate:
  • Approved → proceed to Q2 for classification
  • Not approved → matter retained by LRA for standard processing
Output:
  • Referral approved and proceeds to classification
  • Or matter retained by LRA
  • Action item type updated to Formal Objection or Private Ruling (if required by TARB Referral Officer)

Q2 – How should the matter be classified?

  • Assess correspondence and submitted forms to determine the matter type.
  • Factors indicating Amendment Request (handled by LRS): submitted via My Land Tax or LTX Amendment Form; no supporting documentation; no written reasons; SRO does not dispute; simple error or oversight.
  • Factors indicating Formal Objection (handled by Review): Land Tax Objection Form used; written correspondence challenges the legal basis of the assessment; SRO has previously advised disagreement. All three factors must be present: in writing, grounds stated fully and in detail, challenges the legal basis.
  • Factors indicating Private Ruling (handled by Tax Advisory): amendment/exemption request requiring interpretation of law but does not meet conditions of a Valid Formal Objection; objections to Special Land Tax assessments (disputes rate of tax, not Commissioner's discretion).
  • Some Formal Objections stay with LRA or LGA Team:
    • Valuation objections → forward to LGA Team.
    • Straightforward allow cases with no legal/complex issues → handled by LRA.
  • Out-of-time objections (lodged outside 60-day statutory period) — still referred to Review provided factors are met and reasons for late lodgement are stated.
  • If classification is unclear, seek confirmation from TARB.
Procedure reference: What is an Objection / Amendment?; What do Land Revenue, Review and TAB and some other Policy & Legislation Branches do?; Identifying Formal Objections and Private Rulings
Input data:
  • Customer correspondence (written / MLT / objection form / amendment form)
  • Submitted forms and supporting documentation
  • Prior SRO correspondence or disagreement history
  • Date of lodgement (for out-of-time assessment)
Processing responsibility:
  • LRA staff — assess and classify
  • TARB — confirmation if classification is ambiguous
  • e‑Sys — action item creation and routing
Decision: Matter classification — determines routing:
  • Amendment request → LRS processes
  • Formal Objection → Review Team (via e‑Sys objections module)
  • Formal Objection (valuation) → LGA Team
  • Formal Objection (straightforward allow) → LRA retains
  • Private Ruling → Tax Advisory Team
  • Special Land Tax objection → Tax Advisory Team (not a valid formal objection)
  • Out-of-time objection → Review Team (with late lodgement reasons)
Output:
  • Correct action item type assigned (Amendment / Formal Objection / Private Ruling)
  • Routed to appropriate team queue

Q3 – Does the correspondence constitute a hardship relief application?

  • Confirm whether customer intent to apply for hardship relief is clearly evident in the correspondence.
  • Forward confirmed hardship relief applications to LTX Capture/SRO via email, specifying the Customer ID and requesting a "hardship action item" be created in e‑Sys.
  • The matter will then be recorded via the e‑Sys private rulings module.
  • Do not treat casual hardship mentions (e.g. "paying land tax will cause hardship") as hardship relief applications unless the customer shows clear intent to apply.
  • If unsure, contact TARB for confirmation before forwarding.
Do not treat hardship mentions without clear intent as hardship relief applications.
Procedure reference: Reviewing applications for hardship relief
Input data:
  • Customer correspondence (assessed for explicit hardship relief intent)
  • Customer ID
Processing responsibility:
  • LRA staff — assess intent and forward if confirmed
  • TARB — confirmation if intent is ambiguous
  • LTX Capture / SRO — hardship action item creation in e‑Sys
  • e‑Sys private rulings module — record the matter
Decision: Hardship relief gate:
  • Confirmed hardship application → email LTX Capture/SRO with CID and request
  • Ambiguous → contact TARB for confirmation
  • Not a hardship application → standard processing continues
Output:
  • Hardship action item created in e‑Sys (if confirmed)
  • Matter recorded via private rulings module
  • Or standard processing continues

Q4 – How do I refer the matter to TARB in e‑Sys?

  • Change the Action Item type to "Private Rulings" (for Tax Advisory) or "Formal Objection" (for Review). Alternatively, create a new Action Item.
  • Change "Allocate To" to "LTX Amendment Referrals".
  • Ensure "Reason 1" is accurate and covers all relevant assessment years.
  • Add comments noting the action item has been referred to TARB as either a Private Ruling or Formal Objection, and include the legislative basis for the request.
  • Change status to "Referred to TAB" (Private Rulings) or "Referred to Review" (Objections).
  • Click the "Create Priv Ruling" or "Create Objection" button that becomes active. Click OK on the information box, then OK to close the action item.
  • The Private Ruling / Objection will appear under the "Objections & Private Rulings" node.
  • If flipping an amendment A/I to a Formal Objection aged >3 months (not previously triaged by TARB), email the Review inbox (review@sro.vic.gov.au) so TARBLOs can prioritise triaging.
Procedure reference: Referring a matter to TARB
Input data:
  • Classification result from Q2 (Formal Objection / Private Ruling)
  • Relevant assessment years
  • Legislative basis for the request
  • Action item age (for aged amendment flip notification)
Processing responsibility:
  • LRA staff — update action item fields and trigger referral in e‑Sys
  • e‑Sys — action item type change, allocation, status update, OC/PR creation
  • Review inbox (review@sro.vic.gov.au) — notification for aged flipped objections
Decision: Referral execution:
  • Private Ruling → status "Referred to TAB" → Create Priv Ruling
  • Formal Objection → status "Referred to Review" → Create Objection
  • Aged flip (>3 months) → additional email to Review inbox
Output:
  • Action item updated: type, allocation, Reason 1, comments, status
  • Private Ruling or Objection created in e‑Sys
  • Appears under "Objections & Private Rulings" node
  • Email notification sent for aged flips (if applicable)

Q5 – Has TARB issued a determination requiring processing?

  • Private Rulings: "Private Ruling Complete" workflow items are created by TARB Officers. Double-click the work item to launch the PR. Right-click on the "Resolve" Action Type and select "View Action" to view TARB Officer instructions.
  • Objections: Emails are sent to the Assessing inbox with instructions on amendments required once the determination is made and customer notified.
  • If instructions are unclear, refer to the Private Ruling File Report/Memo or Objection File Report/Memo in CM10. These detail the decision including exemption types and relevant sections of the Land Tax Act 2005.
  • If still unclear after reviewing CM10, clarify with the TARB Officer directly.
  • Make any necessary amendments: remove non-issues, create refunds, reissue assessments.
  • Interest on refunds: Interest generally applies on refunds resulting from objections but not on refunds resulting from private rulings. Check TARB instructions — if assessment was unpaid at determination but subsequently paid, interest may now apply.
  • Correspondence is generally only needed if a refund requires EFT details (TARB has already notified the customer of the outcome). Use the One Note template for NOD correspondence.
Procedure reference: Processing completed TARB matters; Reviewing decision and amendments required
Input data:
  • Private Rulings:
    • "Private Ruling Complete" workflow item
    • "Resolve" Action Type instructions from TARB Officer
    • CM10 Private Ruling File Report/Memo (if instructions unclear)
  • Objections:
    • Email to Assessing inbox with amendment instructions
    • CM10 Objection File Report/Memo (if instructions unclear)
  • Payment status of assessment (for interest determination)
Processing responsibility:
  • LRA staff — review instructions, process amendments, create refunds, reissue assessments
  • e‑Sys — assessment amendments, liability recalculation, non-issue removal
  • CM10 — determination report/memo retrieval
  • TARB Officer — clarification if instructions unclear
Decision: Post-determination action:
  • Amendment required → process changes, refunds, reissued assessments
  • No amendment required → proceed to close
  • Interest on refund: applies for objections (generally); does not apply for private rulings (generally)
Output:
  • Assessments amended in e‑Sys
  • Non-issues removed (if applicable)
  • Refunds created (with or without interest per determination type)
  • Assessments reissued
  • EFT details requested from customer (if refund required)

Q6 – What must be done before closing the matter?

  • If no action item exists, create one (Formal Objection or Private Ruling as applicable) to record notes.
  • Objections — complete the Objection Module: The OC status will be "Resolved". Click the "Action" tab → Edit → right-click → New → select "Complete" from the Action Type LOV → OK → Save.
  • Private Rulings: PRs are generally already in "Completed" status — this step is typically not required.
  • Check and complete any related Workflow items.
  • Formal Objections flipped to Amendments: If TARB flipped a Formal Objection to an amendment A/I for LRA processing and the matter is too complex:
    • Contact a TARBLO / TL / Gr6 to discuss.
    • If agreed to revert, create an Objection (not Private Ruling) in e‑Sys and create the OC shell.
    • Do not contact the customer or representative directly.
    • If lodgement date >6 months, email Review inbox (review@sro.vic.gov.au) for prioritised allocation.
Procedure reference: Post-amendment steps; Formal Objections flipped to Amendments: Flipping back to TARB
Input data:
  • Completed amendments from Q5
  • Objection Module status (for OCs)
  • Private Ruling status (for PRs)
  • Related Workflow items
  • Flipped objection complexity assessment (if applicable)
  • Original lodgement date (for aged flip-back notification)
Processing responsibility:
  • LRA staff — complete modules, close workflow items, create action items if missing
  • e‑Sys — Objection Module status update, workflow item completion
  • TARBLO / TL / Gr6 — consultation for flip-back decisions
  • Review inbox (review@sro.vic.gov.au) — notification for aged flip-backs (>6 months)
Decision: Closure path:
  • Objection → change OC status from "Resolved" to "Completed"
  • Private Ruling → generally already "Completed" (no action)
  • Flipped objection too complex → revert to TARB (create OC shell, notify Review if >6 months)
Output:
  • Objection Module set to "Completed" (for OCs)
  • Action item created with notes (if none existed)
  • All related Workflow items completed
  • Matter closed
  • Or: OC shell created and matter reverted to TARB (for complex flip-backs)

Procedure Summary

  • Defines the process used by LRA staff to assess and refer land tax matters to TARB for determination.
  • Covers classification of matters as amendment requests, formal objections, private rulings, or hardship relief applications.
  • Describes consultation, referral, determination, and post-determination amendment processing activities.
  • Identifies decision points relating to matter classification, referral suitability, and determination handling.
  • Specifies closure activities following determinations issued by TARB.

Team Routing Reference

  • Land Revenue Services (LRS): Factual inaccuracies, general enquiries, simple amendments, information updates
  • Review Team: Formal Objections on legal basis, out-of-time objections, hardship relief, penalty objections, investigation decision objections
  • Tax Advisory Team: Private Rulings, amendment/exemption requests requiring legal interpretation, Special Land Tax objections
  • LGA Team: Valuation objections
  • Legal Services: VCAT / Supreme Court referrals (via memo to Branch Manager, Legal Services)
  • Branch Manager: Ombudsman queries

Key Processing Time Standards

  • Formal Objections: 90 days
  • Private Rulings: 90 days
  • Amendment Requests: 60 days

Key Email Addresses

  • Review inbox: review@sro.vic.gov.au — for aged objection flips, appeals against Review determinations
  • Tax Advisory inbox: tax.advisory@sro.vic.gov.au — for Special Land Tax objections, appeals against Tax Advisory determinations
  • LTX Capture/SRO: hardship action item creation requests

Structural Limitations & Ambiguities

Matter classification: Relies on qualitative assessment of correspondence rather than explicit binary decision rules — may warrant human review.
Sequencing of referral and processing: Order of activities is implied rather than explicitly mandated within the procedure text.
