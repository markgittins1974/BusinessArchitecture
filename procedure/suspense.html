Suspense Management and Refund Procedure Overview

Purpose

Determine appropriate actions for suspense payments and process refunds when customers have overpaid land tax or when amendments result in overpayments.

Key Performance Indicator (KPI)

Refund applications must be completed within 60 days (in accordance with Balanced Scorecard)

Scope

  • Suspense payments awaiting allocation to customer liabilities
  • Overpayment refunds under three sections: 90AA, 24A, 19
  • Interest calculations for refunds from formal objections
  • Refund approval levels and responsibilities
  • Retrospective policy application

Two Main Paths

  • Path A: Allocate Suspense - Suspense payment allocated to outstanding liabilities (no refund issued)
  • Path B: Issue Refund - Customer entitled to refund due to overpayment or amendment (refund issued)
Authority: Land Tax Act 1958, Taxation Administration Act 1997 | KPI: 60 days | Source: Land Revenue Services
Input: Suspense payment received or refund application received
Process: Determine if suspense should be allocated to liability or if refund should be processed
Decision: Allocate to liability OR issue refund? Which refund type (s90AA, s24A, s19)?
Output: Suspense allocated, or refund processed with interest (if applicable), KPI within 60 days

Q1 - Can Suspense Be Allocated to Outstanding Liabilities?

Suspense Allocation Process

When a suspense payment is received, determine if it can be allocated to outstanding liabilities before issuing a refund.

Allocation Rules

  • Overpayments should be allocated to outstanding liabilities (prior years or future years)
  • Can allocate to future year if assessment will be issued that year
  • Customer identity must be certain and unambiguous
  • Check for "Under Investigation" NII - if present, DO NOT allocate

Transfers Between Trusts/Trustees/Beneficiaries

Generally, suspense payments should NOT be transferred between trusts/trustees/beneficiaries WITHOUT permission

Can Transfer WITHOUT Permission:

  • Trustee to Trust (same entity)
  • Trust to Trustee (same entity)

CANNOT Transfer WITHOUT Permission:

  • Joint Ownership to Individual
  • One trust to different trust
  • One trustee to different trustee
  • Beneficiary transfers between entities

Special Cases

Estate Executor

If executor of estate contacts SRO regarding suspense payment on deceased's account, can allocate to beneficiary accounts per executor direction (with proper authority/documentation).

Deregistered Company

If company deregistered in ASIC, transfer suspense to the Principal Place of Business (not registered office). Consider allocating to group if company was member of LTX group.

S105 Certificate

If suspense payment made on S105 certificate where no land tax assessment issued, transfer to Treasury Trust.

If customer under "Under Investigation" NII: DO NOT allocate suspense. Wait for investigation to conclude.

Output: Suspense allocated to liability, or proceed to Q2 for refund path

Q2 - Is Customer Eligible for Refund?

Refund Eligibility Criteria

Customer is eligible for refund if:

  1. Customer has overpaid land tax (paid more than assessment)
  2. Assessment has been amended (due to objection, request, or internal error)
  3. Amendment resulted in reduced liability or overpayment
  4. Minimum refund amount met (varies by refund type)
  5. Refund amount calculated correctly with interest (if applicable)

Investigation Flag Check

CRITICAL: If customer has "Under Investigation" NII for ANY revenue matter, consult Senior Officer before proceeding with refund.

Retrospective Policy and Section 19 of TAA

SRO Retrospective Policy: Refunds of overpaid land tax can be made for payments allocated to assessment years, looking back a full retrospective period (typically 5 years) from date of refund request.

Section 19 of TAA: Commissioner may refund overpayments made on or after 1 June (of the contact year - 5 years).

Example: Retrospective Policy Application

Customer contacts SRO on 1 June 2023 advising they have been paying land tax on property that is their PPR:

  • Retrospective Policy: Can apply PPR exemption from 1 December 2018 (system wide retrospective period)
  • TAA Section 19: Can refund overpayments made on or after 1 June 2018 (5 years back from contact date)
  • Prior to 1 June 2018: Would need to assess if any earlier overpayments eligible outside TAA s19 scope
Reference: Taxation Administration Act 1997 s19, SRO Retrospective Policy
Procedure Detail: Refund Responsibilities, Refund Approval Levels & Handover Procedures▼

Refund Responsibilities

Staff member allocated refund request:

  • Takes full responsibility for evaluation and decision
  • Monitors progress and ensures timely follow-up
  • Informs customer of unexpected delays
  • Ensures refund request completed satisfactorily
  • Ensures all due processes completed

Can refer specific issues to:

  • TARB for formal objection decisions
  • Valuation team for property valuation issues
  • Other specialists for technical matters
  • Referred area assumes responsibility for that specific issue only

Refund Approval Levels

Grade 2/3/4 Mentor approval required for:

  • First refund request by staff member
  • Complex or unusual refund scenarios
  • Refunds involving interest calculations
  • Refunds requiring policy interpretation

Mentor responsibilities:

  • Ensure policies and procedures adhered to
  • Provide formal written decision
  • Ensure adequate documentation on file
  • Formally notify staff member of decision

Handover Between Staff Members

If necessary to transfer responsibility:

  1. Original staff member documents full context in action item notes
  2. New staff member assumes full responsibility from that point
  3. Mentor approves transition
  4. Clear documentation required for audit trail
Input: Customer refund request, assessment history, payment history
Process: Check eligibility (overpaid, assessment amended, minimum met), check investigation status
Decision: Is customer eligible for refund? Apply retrospective policy/TAA s19?
Output: If YES - proceed to Q3; If NO - explain to customer, close request

Q3 - Which Refund Type Applies?

Three Refund Types

Refunds are categorized under sections of Land Tax Act 1958:

Section 90AA - Overpayment (Double Payment or Over-Payment)

Scenario: No amendment; overpaid liability only (double payment or payment above amount due)

Minimum refund amount:

  • $5 if requested by customer
  • $20 if identified internally with no preceding customer request

Interest: Does not apply

Section 24A - Refund from Objection or Amendment Request

Scenario: Amendment made in response to customer request (objection, valuation review, etc.)

Minimum refund amount: $5

Interest: May apply (e.g., successful formal objection or valuation objection)

Section 19 - Refund from Internal Amendment

Scenario: Amendment made internally by identifying and rectifying error; no preceding customer request

Minimum refund amount: $20

Interest: Does not apply

Selection Criteria

  1. Was assessment amended (section 24A or 19) or is it overpayment only (section 90AA)?
  2. Was amendment result of customer objection/request (section 24A) or internal finding (section 19)?
  3. Is refund amount above minimum threshold for that type?
  4. Does formal objection apply? If YES → Interest applies (section 24A only)
Reference: Land Tax Act 1958 sections 19, 24A, 90AA

Successful objection YES YES s24A $5 YES

Section 19 TAA Scope Limits

TAA s19 limits refund scope to 5 years prior to contact date. Example:

  • Customer contacts 1 June 2023
  • TAA s19 scope: 1 June 2018 back
  • Prior years (before 1 June 2018): May need separate assessment under retrospective policy
Input: Assessment amendment history, customer initiation status
Process: Determine if s90AA (no amendment), s24A (customer amendment), or s19 (internal amendment)
Decision: Which refund type? Interest applicable?
Output: Refund type determined, minimum amount verified, interest eligibility determined

Q4 - What Is the Refund Amount Including Interest?

Base Refund Calculation

  1. Identify overpaid amount (payment minus amended assessment)
  2. Verify refund amount exceeds minimum for refund type
  3. If partially allocated to multiple years, allocate most recent payments first
  4. Calculate each year's refund separately if multi-year

Interest Calculations

Interest applies ONLY to refunds resulting from formal objection (section 24A)

Interest Calculation Rules

  • Interest calculated at market rate per section 25 TAA
  • Interest period: From date of payment (overpaid amount) to either:
    • Date of payment of amount overpaid, OR
    • Date Commissioner made assessment subject to objection/review
  • Interest applies ONLY to payments against relevant year of assessment
  • Partial allocations: Most recent payments applied first; interest recalculated for partial refunds
  • Minimum $0.50: No interest payable if less than $0.50 (section 25 TAA)

Example: Interest Calculation

Customer paid 2023 assessment on 15 June 2023 ($5,000). Formal objection successful 15 January 2024, reducing assessment by $1,000. Refund = $1,000 + interest from 15 June 2023 to 15 January 2024 (7 months approx.) at market rate.

Interest ONLY applies to section 24A refunds from successful formal objection. NOT for section 90AA or section 19 refunds.
Reference: Taxation Administration Act 1997 section 25 (market rate)
Procedure Detail: Interest Rate Determination, Multi-Year Refunds & Rounding Rules▼

Interest Rate (Section 25 TAA)

Refer to current market rate determined by Commissioner under TAA s25. Rates update periodically.

Multi-Year Refunds with Interest

When refund spans multiple assessment years:

  1. Calculate refund for each year separately
  2. Calculate interest for each year based on payment dates and objection result date
  3. Combine total refund + total interest

Rounding Rules

  • Round interest to nearest cent (round $0.005 up to $0.01)
  • If interest less than $0.50, interest = $0
  • Show refund amount and interest amount separately on refund advice
Input: Refund type, overpaid amount, assessment amendment date, payment date(s), formal objection date (if applicable)
Process: Calculate base refund, determine if interest applies, calculate interest if eligible
Decision: What is total refund amount (base + interest)? Meets minimum threshold?
Output: Refund amount calculated and verified, interest amount (if applicable) calculated

Q5 - How Should the Refund Be Processed?

Refund Processing Steps

  1. Verify all calculations correct (base amount, interest, total)
  2. Confirm refund type and minimum threshold met
  3. Obtain mentor approval if required (first refund, complex, interest)
  4. Generate refund advice letter for customer
  5. Allocate any remaining balance to future liabilities (if applicable)
  6. Process refund payment in e-Sys (bank transfer, cheque, etc.)
  7. Document outcome in action item notes
  8. Close action item and ensure within 60-day KPI

Refund Advice Letter

Letter should include:

  • Refund amount (base)
  • Interest amount (if applicable)
  • Total refund amount
  • Refund type and basis (s90AA, s24A, s19)
  • Payment method and estimated delivery date
  • Reference to assessment amendment (if applicable)

KPI Compliance

Time standard: 60 days from refund request received to refund processed

Note: If refund created as result of objection/amendment request, KPI may be extended due to time required for review processes.

Reference: Balanced Scorecard KPI, Suspense Management and Refund Procedure
Procedure Detail: e-Sys Refund Processing, Payment Methods & Exemption Scenarios▼

e-Sys Refund Processing

  1. Navigate to customer Refunds node
  2. Click Create Refund
  3. Select refund type (90AA, 24A, or 19)
  4. Enter refund amount (base)
  5. Enter interest amount (if applicable)
  6. Select payment method
  7. Add customer notes with calculation basis
  8. Obtain mentor approval (if required)
  9. Click Process Refund
  10. System generates refund advice letter
  11. Refund appears as processed/pending payment

Payment Methods

  • Bank transfer (most common) - fastest method
  • Cheque - if customer requests or bank details unavailable
  • Offset against future liability (if customer agrees)

Exemption Scenarios

PPR Exemption Applied Retrospectively: If PPR exemption applied to past years, recalculate assessment and refund accordingly. Ensure calculation goes back to system-wide retrospective period date.

Group Membership Changes: If customer was/is member of LTX group, may affect liability calculations and refund scope. Confirm group membership status for all relevant years.

Input: Verified refund amount, interest (if applicable), mentor approval status
Process: Process refund in e-Sys, select payment method, generate refund advice
Decision: Payment method selected? Mentor approval obtained? KPI compliance verified?
Output: Refund processed, advice sent to customer, action item closed within 60-day KPI

Procedure Summary

  • Purpose: Manage suspense payments and process refunds for overpaid land tax
  • Two paths: Allocate suspense to liability OR process refund
  • Q1: Check if suspense can be allocated (avoid refund if possible)
  • Q2: Verify customer eligible (overpaid, amendment made, minimum met)
  • Q3: Determine refund type (90AA, 24A, or 19) based on circumstances
  • Q4: Calculate refund amount, determine if interest applies (24A formal objection only)
  • Q5: Process refund, issue advice letter, close within 60-day KPI
  • Retrospective policy allows refunds for payments up to 5 years back (TAA s19 scope)
  • Interest applies ONLY to section 24A refunds from formal objections
  • Key escalations: Investigation NII, complex scenarios, mentor approval
