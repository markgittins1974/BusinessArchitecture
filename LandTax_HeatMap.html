Land Tax — Heat Maps

Capability and process maturity views across all six value stages — including Common capabilities.

What am I looking at?

This view shows every Land Tax capability organised by the six value stages that make up the end to end Land Tax lifecycle — from maintaining land records (VS1) through to issuing certificates (VS6). Each card represents an L3 capability — the most granular unit of business function. The colour indicates its current maturity: how well established, consistent, and effective that capability is today.

How to read it
Each column is a value stage — a major phase of Land Tax administration. Capabilities are listed top to bottom within each stage. Cards tagged COM are Common capabilities — reusable platform functions (e.g. Customer Management, Property Data) that are shared across multiple value stages.
How to use the filter
Use the maturity buttons above the grid to highlight capabilities at a specific level. High (green) capabilities are stable and effective. Medium (blue) have gaps in execution. Low (yellow) are fragmented, heavily manual, or reliant on workarounds — these are typically priority areas for improvement.

What am I looking at?

This view reorganises the same capabilities by the business process they belong to, rather than listing them individually. Each card represents a named process (e.g. "Administer Land Ownership and Notice of Acquisition") and lists the capabilities that are performed within it. This reveals which processes carry the most capabilities and where maturity is concentrated or lacking.

Work Items
Where a capability has been linked to operational work items, these appear below the capability name in blue. Work items are the discrete tasks that staff perform day to day — e.g. "LOAN - Resolve Party Match" or "PPR". Each shows its KPI target (days to complete) and current items on hand (backlog volume).
Common Processes
Process cards with a lighter header contain Common capabilities — shared platform functions invoked by the value stage. These are distinguished visually so you can see at a glance which processes are stage specific versus shared across Land Tax.
