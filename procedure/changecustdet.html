Change of Customer Details - Procedure Overview

Purpose

Identify and describe the process to complete a Change of Customer Details (COCD) work item for land tax. Involves validating, authorizing, and updating customer information in e-Sys to ensure accurate assessment issuance.

Why It Matters

  • Accurate land tax assessments minimize need for amendments, phone calls, and returned unclaimed assessments
  • Timely processing keeps customer records current
  • Proper authority verification protects customer interests

Types of Changes Handled

  • Change of postal address
  • Change of name
  • Change of contact information (phone, email)
  • Addition or change of authorised representative
  • Change of property details (street name/number)
  • Principal Place of Residence (PPR) amendments

Five Basic Steps (Q1-Q5)

  1. Q1: Launch the COCD work item from e-Sys workflow
  2. Q2: Determine if requester is authorised contact
  3. Q3: Extract and read the change request
  4. Q4: Update customer record in e-Sys
  5. Q5: Complete and close the action item
Time Standard: 7.5 minutes per item | Revised: November 2024
Input: COCD work item in e-Sys workflow, customer request (form/call/email/letter)
Process: Q1-Q5: Launch → Authorize → Extract → Update → Complete
Decision: Is requester authorised? Is change valid? Does PPR apply?
Output: Updated customer record in e-Sys, completed action item

Q1 - Launch COCD Work Item

Accessing the Work Item

  1. Select your work tray in the e-Sys tree (usually in your Home or Tray section)
  2. Locate the COCD action item in your tray
  3. Double-click the item OR select it and click "Launch"
  4. Action item opens in edit mode and customer is loaded into the tree

Identifying the Source

While action item is open:

  1. Select the Enquiry tab at the top
  2. Look for the red circle icon which indicates the source type
  3. Source types help determine where to find the actual request (see Summary section for details)

Source Types at a Glance

  • SmartForm (Gen 03): Change of Customer Details form submitted via web
  • My Land Tax: Update submitted through customer portal
  • Written: Letter or written correspondence
  • Phone: Request made via telephone
Note: If the COCD item type is incorrect (e.g., request is for something other than above), refer to a Senior Officer as the work item type may need changing and redirecting.
Procedure Detail: Understanding Action Item Source Icons▼

Enquiry Tab Information

The Enquiry tab shows the source of the work item via a red circle icon:

  • Form icon: SmartForm (Gen 03) submission
  • Website icon: My Land Tax submission
  • Letter icon: Written correspondence
  • Phone icon: Phone call inquiry

Importance of Identifying Source

The source type determines where to locate the customer's original request—in e-Sys document management (CM10), My Land Tax records, correspondence file, or call notes.

Input: COCD work item in staff member's e-Sys workflow tray
Process: Staff launches item from tray, e-Sys loads customer into tree
Decision: Is work item correct type? Is source identifiable?
Output: Action item open, source type identified, ready for Q2

Q2 - Determine if Requester is Authorised

Authority Determination Process

Key Rule: Only customers and automatically authorised contacts can request changes. All other parties need written authority from the customer.

Step 1: Determine Customer Type

In e-Sys, navigate to Maintain Customer screen to check customer type:

  • Individual: The person whose name appears on the account
  • Company: Registered business entity (ABN/ACN)
  • Trust: Trustee-held entity
  • Joint Ownership (JO): Multiple customers sharing ownership
  • Estate: Deceased person's property
  • Land Tax Group: Related companies with common directors

Step 2: Check if Requester is Automatically Authorised

AUTOMATICALLY AUTHORISED:

  • The Taxpayer/Customer (or any JO member)
  • Legal Personal Representatives (Executors, Power of Attorney holders)
  • Company Senior Officers (Directors, Secretaries, CFOs, Managers)
  • Company Officer with relevant company email address
  • Trustee (Individual or Company)
  • Previously listed authorised representatives (check Contact tab)

NOT AUTOMATICALLY AUTHORISED (Need written authority):

  • Personal Accountant
  • External Accountant
  • Solicitor/Lawyer
  • Managing Agent
  • Real Estate Agent
  • Family Members

Step 3: Check for Previously Authorised Representatives

In e-Sys:

  1. Navigate to Land Taxpayer node
  2. Select Contact tab
  3. Review list of previously authorised representatives
  4. If requester is listed, they are authorised
CRITICAL: If requester is NOT automatically authorised AND NOT previously listed, you MUST have written signed authority from customer before proceeding with any changes.
Authority Letter Requirements: Must be signed and dated by customer/director, state representative is authorised to interact with SRO, and acknowledge all properties now and in future included in land tax notices.
Procedure Detail: Authority Letter Handling▼

If Unauthorised Representative Provides Authority Letter

Proceed with the request. Authority letter may be attached to SmartForm or included separately.

If Unauthorised Representative Does NOT Provide Authority Letter

1. Email representative requesting authority letter (template at end of procedure)

2. Reserve the action item for 2 weeks

3. If no reply within 2 weeks, complete action item WITHOUT making requested update

What We Do NOT Accept

Management Authority documents are NOT accepted as authority letters for COCD requests.

Adding Previously Unauthorised Representative to Contact List

If authority letter provided and representative is new:

  1. Navigate to Land Taxpayer Node
  2. Click Edit icon
  3. Select Contact tab
  4. Right-click and select "Add Contact"
  5. Enter: Name, Organisation, Position/Role, Contact Type, Phone, Email
  6. Tick the "Gen 03" box
  7. Click OK and Save
Input: Customer type, requester identity, source of request
Process: Assess if requester is automatically authorised; if not, check for written authority
Decision: Is requester authorised to make changes?
Output: AUTHORISED (proceed) or UNAUTHORISED (request authority or close item)

Q3 - Extract and Read Change Request

Finding the Request (By Source Type)

SmartForm (Gen 03) Requests

Best Practice Method:

  1. Cancel out of the COCD action item
  2. Select the CM10 icon on e-Sys tree (document management)
  3. Select the Land Tax folder (identified by customer number/LTX)
  4. Right-click on the relevant document(s)
  5. Select View from menu to open SmartForm

Alternative: Open action item, select Other tab to view document directly.

My Land Tax Requests

Access the My Land Tax system to locate customer's submission and review changes requested.

Written Correspondence

Check correspondence files or attached documents in e-Sys document management for letter.

Phone Call Requests

Review call notes in action item or customer record notes.

Reading SmartForm

Page 1: Declaration (customer agreement—no relevant information needed)

Page 2: Person completing form details (confirm email/phone with e-Sys records)

Page 3: Customer details and change request (what changes are being requested)

Attachments: Authority letters or supporting documents

Key Information to Extract

  • What change is being requested? (address, name, phone, email, representative, property details)
  • Which revenue lines does this apply to?
  • Is this request from customer or representative?
  • Are there any authority letters attached?
  • Is a PPR (Principal Place of Residence) amendment being requested or affected?
Note: If customer selected multiple revenue lines in form, you can process normally. If customer has additional revenue lines not selected, consult Senior Officer about whether to update those as well.
Input: Source type (form/call/letter/My LTX), document location
Process: Locate and retrieve original customer request, extract key change details
Decision: Are all requested changes identifiable and documented?
Output: Clear understanding of what changes customer is requesting

Q4 - Update Customer Record in e-Sys

Update Contact Information

If person completing form provided phone or email:

  1. Navigate to Maintain Customer screen
  2. Click the Edit icon (yellow pencil)
  3. Update phone and/or email fields
  4. Click Save icon (yellow floppy disc)

Verify Customer Details

If customer is Individual: Check date of birth against form

If customer is Company/Trust: Check ACN/ABN matches, verify in ASIC if needed and update

Update Mailing Address

Updating mailing address varies by customer type:

  • Individual: Update address in Maintain Customer screen
  • Company: Update address in Maintain Customer screen
  • Trust: Update address in appropriate trust screen
  • Joint Ownership: May need to update for specific JO member
  • Estate: Update per estate-specific procedures
  • Land Tax Group: Update for group as defined

Adding New Authorised Representative

If customer requested addition of new representative:

  1. Navigate to Land Taxpayer Node
  2. Click Edit icon
  3. Select Contact tab
  4. Right-click and select Add Contact
  5. Enter: Name, Organisation, Position/Role, Contact Type, Phone, Email
  6. Tick the "Gen 03" box in corner
  7. Click OK and Save

Principal Place of Residence (PPR) Amendments

Applying PPR: ALL criteria must be met:

  • Property is owned by customer
  • Property Classification eligible for PPR exemption
  • Customer updated residential address with VicRoads (COI/DLS)
  • No active RTBA bond on property
  • Apply from 1 December current year (exemption effective next year)

Removing PPR: Must verify customer not residing at property:

  • If RTBA bond exists: Remove from 1/12 current year
  • If no bond: Check VicRoads (COI/DLS) address updated. If yes, remove from 1/12
  • Remove from 1/12 current year so exemption doesn't apply to next year's assessment
CRITICAL: Be certain customer not residing at property before removing PPR. Check VicRoads residential address first.
Input: Requested changes from customer (address, name, contact info, representative, PPR)
Process: Update appropriate e-Sys screens based on customer type and change type
Decision: Are all changes entered correctly? PPR criteria met?
Output: Updated customer record in e-Sys

Q5 - Complete and Close Action Item

Final Verification

Before closing, verify:

  • All requested changes have been entered in e-Sys
  • Changes match what customer requested
  • PPR criteria verified (if applicable)
  • All contact information updated
  • New representatives added (if applicable)

Completing the Action Item

  1. Navigate back to the COCD action item
  2. Review all changes one final time
  3. Add any notes to the action item explaining changes made
  4. Set status to Completed or Closed (depending on e-Sys workflow)
  5. Click Save and Close or Complete button

Action Item Completion Note

Document in the action item notes what was changed, for example:

  • "Updated mailing address to [new address]"
  • "Added John Smith as authorised representative"
  • "Applied PPR exemption from 1/12/2024"
  • "Verified authority letter from xyz"
Time Standard Met: Action completed within 7.5 minutes (7 minutes 30 seconds) from launch to close.
Procedure Detail: Items Requiring Senior Officer Referral▼

Situations to Escalate

If any of the following occur, refer to Senior Officer BEFORE completing:

  • COCD work item type appears incorrect
  • Requester not automatically authorised and no authority letter provided
  • Conflicting information across different sources
  • Customer has additional revenue lines not selected on request
  • PPR criteria not fully met but customer expecting exemption
  • Concerns about validity of authority letter

How to Escalate

1. Add detailed notes to action item explaining issue

2. Leave item in "Pending Review" or "On Hold" status

3. Email Senior Officer with item reference number and details

4. Await guidance before completing

Input: Updated e-Sys records with all changes entered
Process: Final verification of changes, documentation of actions taken
Decision: Are all changes correct and complete?
Output: Completed action item, closed in e-Sys workflow, customer records updated

Principal Place of Residence (PPR) Rules

Applying PPR Exemption

ALL the following criteria MUST be met:

  • Property is owned by customer
  • Property Classification code eligible for PPR exemption
  • Customer updated residential address with VicRoads (COI/DLS)
  • No active RTBA bond data on property
  • Apply from 1 December current year (exemption takes effect following year)

Example: If customer contacts on 3 March 2024, PPR applied from 1/12/2024, exemption effective 2025 assessment.

Removing PPR Exemption

Critical Rule: Be very sure customer is NOT residing at property before removing PPR.

If property has active RTBA bond:

  • Remove PPR from 1/12 current year
  • Exemption does not apply to following year's assessment

If property has NO active bond:

  • Check if VicRoads (COI/DLS) residential address updated
  • If updated, remove PPR from 1/12 current year
  • Exemption does not apply to following year's assessment

Key Terms

Authorised Contact: Person with authority to request changes on customer's behalf (customer, legal rep, company officer, trustee, or previously authorised rep)
Unauthorised Party: Person without automatic authority (accountant, solicitor, agent, family) who needs written customer authority
Authority Letter: Signed document from customer permitting another person to interact with SRO on their behalf
PPR Exemption: Tax exemption for principal place of residence (main home)
Revenue Lines: Different tax types customer may be assessed for (e.g., LTX, VRLT)
