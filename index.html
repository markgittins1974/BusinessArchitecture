SRO Transformation Architecture

Value Stream Maps

Understand how SRO delivers Land Tax through interconnected value streams, business capabilities, and strategic initiatives. Select a map to explore the capabilities that underpin each value stage.

Core Concept
What is a Value Stream?
A value stream is a sequence of activities that deliver customer value. In Land Tax, each value stream represents a critical business outcome—from maintaining accurate records to resolving disputes.
Six value streams guide Land Tax delivery: Maintain, Determine, Assess, Manage, Resolve, and Certify.
Core Concept
What is a Business Capability?
A business capability is a repeatable unit of work that delivers value. Capabilities can be common (shared across value streams), supporting (governance, enabling), or value-stage specific (delivering Land Tax outcomes).
Each capability has a defined process, policy reference, and maturity level—from Initial through Optimised.
Strategic Framework
Business Process
Business processes define the end-to-end workflows that deliver Land Tax outcomes. They orchestrate capabilities across value streams and ensure consistent, compliant execution.
Use business processes to design workflows, define handoffs, and optimise operational efficiency.
How to Use This
Navigating the Maps
Start with a value stream chevron to see its capabilities. Toggle between Common and Supporting lenses. Expand capability groups to view synonyms, policy references, and process details. Use the maps for roadmap planning, stakeholder communication, and capability assessments.
All maps are interactive. Click to explore, filter by lens, and print for offline reference.
