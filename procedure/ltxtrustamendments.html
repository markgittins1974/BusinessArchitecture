```
How to Determine Trust Type

  1. Review Trust Deed: Look for clauses defining beneficiary election (discretionary) vs fixed beneficiaries (fixed) vs units (unit trust)
  2. Court Orders: If family court or other court order created the trust, refer to order. Likely implied/constructive trust.
  3. Superannuation: If SMSF, ADF, or PST (and registered), classify as "Excluded Trust"
  4. Deceased Estate: If created by will, classify as "Administration Trust" and verify death date (must be on/after 12 Dec 2007)
  5. If Uncertain: Escalate to Grade 3 with trust deed copy

Special Note: Bare Trust (SMSF with LRBA)

Self-managed super fund purchasing property under Limited Recourse Borrowing Arrangement uses a "bare trust" structure:

  • Bare trustee (entity on title) = Fixed Trust
  • SMSF (ultimate beneficial owner) = Notified beneficiary
  • Assessed at general rates (beneficiary is registered superannuation fund)

General Notification Rules (All Trust Types)

Notification Requirement: All land held on trust must be notified to SRO within ONE MONTH of acquisition.

Grace Period: No penalty if notified within the CALENDAR YEAR of acquisition.

Failure to Notify: Results in Trust Notification Default (TND) penalty under TAA 1997 s29 (if event occurred on/after 1 Jan 2010).

Notification Eligibility by Trust Type

Trust Type Can Notify? Form Effect if Notified Can Withdraw?
Discretionary NO — cannot notify beneficiaries Form 15 (pre-2006 only) If pre-2006 beneficiary nominated: general rates (not surcharge) YES — revoke nomination (applies next tax year)
Fixed YES — optional Form 19 (beneficial interests) Trustee and notified beneficiaries assessed at general rates YES — but cannot re-notify (CRITICAL: WITHDRAWN)
Unit YES — optional (OR nominate PPR, not both) Form 19 (unit holdings) Trustee and notified unitholders assessed at general rates YES — but cannot re-notify (CRITICAL: WITHDRAWN)
Excluded NO — not required N/A Already at general rates (surcharge exempt) N/A
Administration YES — must notify start and completion Form 13 / Form 14 General rates during concessionary period only N/A — automatic end on completion

CRITICAL: Notification Withdrawal Consequences (GAP REMEDIATED)

For Fixed and Unit Trusts ONLY: Once a notification is withdrawn, the trustee CANNOT LODGE ANOTHER NOTIFICATION for that trust going forward. This is a permanent, one-way decision.

Impact: If trustee withdraws notification (e.g., beneficiary changes), trust reverts to surcharge rates permanently. Confirm beneficiary/unitholder intentions BEFORE accepting withdrawal.

PPR Beneficiary Nomination Rules (GAP REMEDIATED)

Eligible Trusts: Discretionary and Unit trusts only.

Eligibility Requirements (MISSING FROM DCPR):

  • Nominated beneficiary MUST use and occupy land as their PPR
  • Cannot use land to conduct substantial business activity
  • Must occupy during relevant PPR period:
    • At least 1 July to 31 December of year before assessment year, OR
    • If acquisition after 1 July: sometime by 31 December (i.e., any time in year before assessment year)
  • Cannot apply retrospectively (even if beneficiary used land as PPR in prior years)
  • Each trust may nominate only ONE PPR beneficiary at a time
  • IMPORTANT: Unit and Discretionary trusts NOT eligible for contiguous PPR exemption (car parks, storage cages)

Effect if Nominated: PPR land assessed at general rates (single holding basis). Nominated beneficiary NOT assessed on PPR land.

Form: Form 19 (PPR beneficiary nomination)

Timing: Takes effect for tax year in which nomination is lodged (if occupancy requirements met).

Cessation: If nominated beneficiary dies or ceases to occupy as PPR, trustee must notify SRO within one month. Penalty applies if notification is late.

Unit Trust Exclusive Choice (GAP REMEDIATED)

CRITICAL CONSTRAINT: Unit trusts must choose EITHER (a) notify unit holders OR (b) nominate PPR beneficiary. They CANNOT DO BOTH.

Confirm which pathway is in force before accepting further amendments.

Procedure Detail: Trust Notification Default & Penalty Calculation (REMEDIATED GAP)▼

CRITICAL GAP REMEDIATED: PENALTY CALCULATION

The DCPR identified penalties but provided no calculation method. This section restores the complete penalty calculation framework.

What Triggers a Trust Notification Default (TND)?

Definition: Failure to lodge a required trust notification under section 46K of the Land Tax Act.

Applicable Events (on/after 1 Jan 2010 only):

  1. Trustee of trust acquiring land to hold on trust (including personal representative of deceased estate)
  2. Changes to trust type (e.g., Discretionary to Unit, Fixed to Discretionary)
  3. Changes in beneficial interest in fixed trust (where beneficial interest notification in force)
  4. Changes in unit holdings in unit trust (where unit holdings notification in force)
  5. Personal representative completing administration of deceased estate

NO TND if: Event occurred before 1 January 2010 OR notification made within calendar year of event (grace period).

When to Recalculate: Timeline Rules

Notification Timing Deadline Grace Period Penalty applies if late?
Acquisition of land 1 month from acquisition date Within same calendar year of acquisition NO penalty if within calendar year; YES after 31 Dec
Change in trust type/beneficiary 1 month from change date Within same calendar year of change NO penalty if within calendar year; YES after 31 Dec
Estate completion 1 month from completion date Within same calendar year of completion NO penalty if within calendar year; YES after 31 Dec

Trust Notification Default Penalty Calculation Formula (CRITICAL)

Methodology: Penalty is imposed on the "additional amount of tax" (AAT) that the taxpayer would have been liable for had the TND not occurred.

Formula:

AAT = A - B - C
```

WHERE:
A = Total new liability for defaulting party (after amendment)
B = Aggregated original proportional liability for the default lands
C = New liability for the non-default lands

OR (if all lands transferred are default lands):
AAT = Total original liability of defaulting party

Important Note: Amount ‘C’ occurs when ALL lands transferred are trust notification default lands (i.e., penalty results in trust liability/ies, not individual liabilities).

Example Calculation

Scenario: Fixed Trust acquired land on 1 Feb 2023, did NOT notify, then notified on 15 Aug 2024 (within calendar year 2023, so no penalty… wait, no — notified in 2024, after 31 Dec 2023, so penalty applies).

  • A (Total new liability for trustee after notification): $5,000 (2023 + 2024 combined)
  • B (Original proportional liability for default land): $2,500 (what they would have owed if had notified on time)
  • C (New liability for non-default lands): $1,000 (other trust lands not affected by this amendment)
  • AAT = $5,000 - $2,500 - $1,000 = $1,500
  • Penalty Tax = $1,500 (equal to AAT)

How to Apply Penalty in e-Sys

  1. Calculate AAT using formula above
  2. Open trust customer liability record in e-Sys
  3. Navigate to “Other” tab or “Penalties” section (exact location varies by e-Sys version)
  4. Create new penalty entry: select “Trust Notification Default” penalty type
  5. Enter calculated AAT amount as penalty amount
  6. Set effective date to the date notification should have been lodged (1 month after event)
  7. Save. Penalty is applied to assessment.

Documentation: Record penalty calculation details in action item comments with formula breakdown so auditors can verify.

Revenue Impact Principle

Penalty guidelines specify consideration of Revenue Impact Principle: Penalty should not result in taxpayer being double-taxed. Use deductions to avoid double taxation where appropriate.

Source: Land Tax Act 2005 s46K; Taxation Administration Act 1997 s29; DOC/19/1084164 Penalty Tax in e-Sys for Land Tax
Input data: Notification dates, trust event dates, current liability records
Processing responsibility: Staff (default determination and penalty calculation), e-Sys (penalty entry and application)
Decision: Trust Notification Default applies (YES/NO)
Output: Manual penalty calculated and applied (if YES), OR no penalty (if NO); proceed to Q5

```
Procedure Detail: Liability Recalculation & Suspense Rules (REMEDIATED GAP)▼

SIGNIFICANT GAPS REMEDIATED

The DCPR provided minimal guidance on recalculation scope and suspense allocation. This detail restores the complete recalculation and suspense framework.

Scope of Recalculation: All Affected Customers

Mandatory: Recalculate liabilities for ALL of the following (where applicable):

  • Trustee: All assessment years from acquisition to present
  • All Beneficiaries/Unitholders: If notified or nominated, recalculate their individual assessments
  • Joint Owners: If property held by trustee and joint owner, recalculate both customer accounts
  • Related Trusts: If amendment affects linked trusts (e.g., sub-trust look-through), recalculate all layers

Years to Recalculate: From acquisition date through current assessment year (not retrospective beyond property's land tax history).

Discretionary Trust Pre-2006 Land Formula (CRITICAL GAP REMEDIATED)

If amending a Discretionary Trust with BOTH pre-2006 and post-2006 land, use this formula:

Liability = [(General rate × Total land value) × (Pre-2006 value ÷ Total value)]
       + [(Surcharge rate × Total land value) × (Post-2006 value ÷ Total value)]

Example: Discretionary Trust with nominated beneficiary owns $500k pre-2006 land and $500k post-2006 land (total $1M):

  • General rate on $1M = $3,000
  • Surcharge rate on $1M = $5,000
  • Liability = [($3,000) × (0.5)] + [($5,000) × (0.5)] = $1,500 + $2,500 = $4,000

Suspense Payment Reallocation Rules (CRITICAL GAP REMEDIATED)

Absolute Rule: Cannot transfer suspense without written customer PERMISSION to the following:

  • Trust ↔ Beneficiaries (either direction)
  • Between related trusts

Exception: CAN transfer without permission within linked trust structure (e.g., Trustee → Linked Trust customer).

Conditions for Allocation:

  1. Suspense can ONLY be allocated against a previously issued liability (not new assessments)
  2. If allocating suspense to a NEW liability created by amendment, obtain customer authorization first
  3. If liability has not yet issued, obtain authorization before allocating suspense

Documentation: Record in action item what suspense was transferred, between which customers, and whether authorization was obtained.

Outcome Categories

Outcome Definition Action in e-Sys
Amended Existing liability recalculated and new amount is different (higher or lower) Update assessment. If lower, create refund. If higher, issue amended assessment and collect additional.
Withdrawn Existing liability is removed entirely (e.g., property decommissioned or trust dissolved) Mark assessment as withdrawn. Route any suspense to refund account for that customer.
New Created New liability issued (e.g., beneficiary becomes separately assessed for first time, or new trust created) Create new assessment. Allocate suspense only if customer authorized OR suspense was paid against existing liability now split.
```

```
Case Studies

Case Study 1: Fixed Trust with Beneficial Interest Notification (Accepted)

Scenario: Fixed Trust owns land, customer lodges Form 19 notifying beneficial interests of two beneficiaries (50/50 split).

  • Q1: No investigation flag → Proceed
  • Q2: Trust deed confirms fixed beneficiaries → Fixed Trust classification
  • Q3: Form 19 lodged, notification in force → General rates pathway applies
  • Q4: Notification made within one month of acquisition → No TND penalty
  • Q5: Recalculate: Trustee assessed at general rates. Each beneficiary (50/50) assessed at general rates on their proportionate share. Suspense $1,000 from prior surcharge rate assessment reallocated to beneficiaries (authorized) → Amended assessments issued
  • Q6: Email sent to both beneficiaries with new assessment amounts and effective date → Work item completed
  • Outcome: ✓ Amendment processed successfully

Case Study 2: Discretionary Trust Pre-2006 Land with Form 15 (Penalty Applies)

Scenario: Discretionary Trust acquired pre-2006 land, form 15 nomination was not lodged on time (late submission). Trustee now submitting Form 15 in 2024 for land acquired in 2020.

  • Q1: No investigation → Proceed
  • Q2: Trust deed confirms discretionary with unilateral trustee discretion → Discretionary Trust
  • Q3: Form 15 attempted (pre-2006 land), but submission is late (4 years late, outside three-month window) → Nomination is NOT eligible. Revert to surcharge rates for pre-2006 land.
  • Q4: Event (attempted nomination) occurred after 1 Jan 2010 and outside grace period → Check if TND applies to acquisition event itself (2020 acquisition, but notification never made) → Yes, TND applies. Calculate AAT: If assessment was $2,000 at surcharge and would have been $1,200 at general, AAT = $800 → Apply penalty
  • Q5: Recalculate: Trust remains at surcharge rates (nomination ineligible). Liability stays $2,000 + $800 penalty = $2,800. Suspense $500 from overpayment during investigation period allocated to penalty → Amended assessment
  • Q6: Email sent explaining why nomination could not be accepted and that penalty applies → Work item completed
  • Outcome: ✓ Appropriate escalation applied; customer understands deadline requirements

Case Study 3: Unit Trust with Withdrawal of Unitholder Notification (Permanent Consequence)

Scenario: Unit Trust has notified unitholders and been assessed at general rates. Unitholder changes occur, and trustee requests withdrawal of notification.

  • Q1: No investigation → Proceed
  • Q2: Trust deed confirms unit trust structure → Unit Trust
  • Q3: Unitholder notification in force → General rates pathway currently applies. Trustee requests withdrawal of notification.
  • CRITICAL DECISION: Confirm with trustee: "Are you sure you want to withdraw the notification? Once withdrawn, you CANNOT lodge another notification for this unit trust going forward. All future assessments will be at SURCHARGE rates."
  • Q4: Withdrawal event occurred after 1 Jan 2010 → TND applies. Calculate AAT for withdrawal. Old liability at general rates was $3,000. New liability at surcharge rates will be $5,000. AAT = $5,000 - $3,000 = $2,000. Penalty applies.
  • Q5: Recalculate: Prior unitholder assessments withdrawn (each individual assessment closed). Trustee reverts to surcharge rate assessment $5,000. Penalty $2,000 applied. Suspense payments cannot be transferred from individual unitholder accounts to trust without authorization → Seek authorization from trustee for any reallocations
  • Q6: Email sent to trustee explaining permanent consequence of withdrawal and confirming penalty applies → Work item completed
  • Outcome: ✓ Appropriate escalation; customer fully informed of irreversible consequences

Case Study 4: Discretionary Trust with PPR Nomination (Occupancy Verification Fails)

Scenario: Discretionary Trust lodges Form 19 (PPR nomination) for beneficiary who claims to occupy property as PPR, but records show business operates from the address.

  • Q1: No investigation → Proceed

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What's the time limit for processing a trust amendment?

A: Time standard is 35 minutes per work item. This includes investigation check, trust classification, notification verification, penalty calculation (if applicable), liability recalculation, and correspondence.

Q: What if the customer is under investigation?

A: Do not process the amendment. Reassign the work item to the Investigator (find their name in CM9), notify them via email with the investigation number, and close your work item.

Q: How do I know which trust type applies?

A: Consult the Trust Type Taxonomy in the Summary section. Review the trust deed for key clauses: Does trustee have discretion on beneficiaries (discretionary)? Are beneficiaries fixed (fixed)? Are there tradable units (unit trust)? Does a court order apply (implied/constructive)? Escalate to Grade 3 if uncertain.

Q: Can a unit trust both notify unitholders AND nominate a PPR beneficiary?

A: NO. Unit trusts must choose one or the other, not both. This is an exclusive election. Confirm which pathway is in force.

Q: What happens if I withdraw a notification from a fixed trust?

A: The notification is withdrawn and the trust reverts to surcharge rates. CRITICAL: Once withdrawn, the trustee CANNOT lodge another notification for that trust ever again. Confirm the trustee understands this permanent consequence before accepting withdrawal.

Q: How do I calculate a Trust Notification Default penalty?

A: Use the formula: AAT = A - B - C, where:
A = Total new liability for defaulting party
B = Aggregated original proportional liability for default lands
C = New liability for non-default lands
See Q4 Procedure Detail section for worked example.

Q: Can I allocate suspense from the trustee account to the beneficiary account without permission?

A: NO. Suspense cannot be transferred between trust and beneficiaries without written customer authorization. Obtain permission before reallocating. Exception: Can transfer within linked trust structure (e.g., Trustee to Linked Trust) without permission.

Q: What should I include in the action item comments?

A: Include: (1) What you did, (2) Result, (3) Money transferred/refunded, (4) Effect to assessments (withdrawn/amended/new), (5) Correspondence method, (6) User ID, (7) Date. See Q6 Procedure Detail for three detailed examples.

Q: Should I send correspondence via email or letter?

A: Email (via AD/MLT) is preferred and automatically logged in CM10. Use letter only if no email address available, customer requested written notice, or amendment is complex and needs detailed explanation.
```
